
# Spaghetti Bolognese

**Serves:** 5

**Cook:** 2.5-3 hours (slow simmer)

??? note "Ingredients"

    ??? info "Sauce"

        - 1½ tbsp olive oil
        - 2 garlic cloves, minced
        - Pre-cooked frozen soffritto (carrot/celery/onion portion)
        - 500g beef mince
        - ½ cup (125ml) beef stock (liquid)
        - 2 beef bouillon cubes, crumbled
        - 800g can crushed tomatoes
        - 2 tbsp tomato paste
        - 1.5-2 tsp soy sauce
        - 2 tsp white sugar (adjust to taste)
        - 2 dried bay leaves
        - ½ tsp dried thyme
        - 6 bird eye chillies
        - ¾ tsp salt
        - ½ tsp black pepper
        - Red wine vinegar (1-2 tsp if needed)

    ??? info "Per serving"

        - 80g dried spaghetti
        - Parmesan and parsley (optional)

??? note "Method"

    1. **Brown beef:** Heat oil over medium heat. Turn heat to high, add beef mince. Break up and cook until browned, about 2 minutes.

    2. **Sauté:** Add frozen soffritto and garlic, cook 2-3 minutes until heated through and any moisture evaporates.

    3. **Deglaze:** Add beef stock, bring to simmer, scraping bottom of pot. Cook 1 minute.

    4. **Add everything:** Add crushed tomatoes, tomato paste, bouillon cubes, soy sauce, sugar, bay leaves, thyme, salt, and pepper. Stir well, add ¾ cup water.

    5. **Slow simmer:** Bring to simmer, cover with lid, reduce to lowest heat. Cook 2-2.5 hours, stirring every 30 minutes. Surface should barely ripple with occasional bubbles.

    6. **Reduce:** Remove lid, simmer uncovered 20 minutes to thicken. Taste—add red wine vinegar (1-2 tsp) if it needs brightness, more salt if needed.

    7. **Portion:** Divide into 5 portions. Freeze 4, use 1 fresh (or adjust based on servings tonight).

    ??? info "Tossing with pasta (per portion)"

        1. Cook 80g pasta in salted boiling water, MINUS 1 minute from packet time.

        2. Reserve 1 cup pasta water, drain pasta.

        3. Heat 1 sauce portion in large skillet over medium heat. Add pasta and 2-3 tbsp pasta water.

        4. Toss gently 1.5-2 minutes until sauce thickens, becomes glossy, and clings to spaghetti. Add more pasta water if needed.
