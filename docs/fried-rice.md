# Simple Fried Rice with Chicken and Egg

??? note "Ingredients"
    - 150g frozen diced chicken
    - 1 cup cooked basmati rice, cooled (preferably day-old) (structure, base)
    - 2 eggs (protein, richness)
    - 2 tbsp avocado oil (cooking fat, high heat tolerance)
    - 2 cloves garlic, minced (aromatic, flavor base)
    - 1/2 onion, finely diced (aromatic, sweetness)
    - 1/4 cup frozen corn (sweetness, color)
    - 2 spring onions, sliced (fresh flavor, garnish)
    - 1 tbsp soy sauce (umami, salt)
    - 1 tsp sesame oil, if available (nutty aroma, finishing flavor)
    - Salt and pepper to taste (seasoning)

??? note "Preparation"

    Mise en Place
    1. Thaw chicken if frozen (can use microwave on defrost setting if needed)
    2. Mince garlic and dice onion, place in small bowl together
    3. Slice spring onions, separating white/light green parts from dark green tops
    4. Measure out frozen corn in a small bowl
    5. Beat eggs in a bowl with a pinch of salt and pepper
    6. Measure soy sauce into a small bowl or ramekin
    7. Ensure rice is separated and not clumped (break up any clumps with your fingers)

??? note "Cooking Instructions"
    1. Heat your wok over high heat until very hot
    2. Add 1 tbsp avocado oil and swirl to coat
    3. Add chicken pieces and stir-fry until golden and cooked through (about 3-4 minutes)
    4. Remove chicken to a plate and set aside
    5. Add remaining 1 tbsp oil to wok
    6. Pour in beaten eggs and let set for 5-10 seconds, then scramble quickly
    7. When eggs are about 80% cooked but still slightly wet, add garlic, onion, and white parts of spring onion
    8. Stir-fry for 30 seconds until aromatic
    9. Add rice and break up any clumps with your spatula
    10. Stir-fry for 2 minutes, allowing rice to toast slightly
    11. Add frozen corn and cooked chicken back to the wok
    12. Drizzle soy sauce around the edges of the wok (this creates steam and flavor)
    13. Toss everything together for 1-2 minutes until well combined and heated through
    14. Turn off heat, add sesame oil if using
    15. Garnish with green parts of spring onions
    Enhancements and Tips
    - Rice preparation: For best results, cook rice the day before and refrigerate overnight. This dries it out slightly, preventing clumping and giving better texture.
    - Wok technique: Keep ingredients moving constantly. A proper stir-fry should take less than 10 minutes of cooking time.
    - Heat management: Your wok should be hot enough that ingredients sizzle immediately when added.
    - Flavor boost: Add 1/2 tsp of your ginger paste for extra dimension.
    - Texture variation: Add a handful of cashews for crunch.
    - Vegetable additions: Baby spinach or diced capsicum would work well if you have them.
    Plating Presentation
    Serve in a bowl with a sprinkle of green onions on top. For restaurant-style presentation, pack the rice into a small bowl and invert onto a plate to create a dome shape.
