# Mushroom Risotto

**Author:** Nagi  
**Serves:** 4–5  
**Prep time:** 15 minutes  
**Cook time:** 35 minutes  
**Total time:** 50 minutes  

Creamy mushroom risotto loaded with garlicky, buttery mushrooms, with some stirred through and some reserved for the top.

??? note "Ingredients"

    ??? info "Mushrooms"
        - 500 g mushrooms, sliced about 3 mm thick
        - 250 g mushrooms, quartered
        - 3 tbsp butter
        - 2 tbsp olive oil
        - 2 garlic cloves, minced
        - 1/2 tsp salt
        - 1/2 tsp black pepper

    ??? info "Risotto"
        - 1 tbsp butter
        - 1 garlic clove, minced
        - 2 eschallots / French onions, finely chopped  
          - or 1/2 onion, finely chopped
        - 1/4 cup dry white wine
        - 1 1/4 cups arborio rice
        - 5 cups chicken stock, warm

    ??? info "Finishing"
        - 1/2 cup cream, optional
        - 2 tbsp butter, optional
        - 1/2 cup parmesan, finely grated
        - 1/2 tsp salt
        - 1/2 tsp black pepper

    ??? info "To serve"
        - Finely chopped parsley or chives
        - Extra parmesan

??? note "Method"

    ??? info "1. Cook the mushrooms"
        1. Heat 1 tbsp oil and half the butter in a large heavy-based pot over high heat.
        2. Add half the mushrooms and cook until well golden, about 4 minutes.
        3. Add half the garlic, plus 1/4 tsp salt and 1/4 tsp pepper. Cook another 1–2 minutes until golden.
        4. Remove to a bowl.
        5. Repeat with the remaining mushrooms.

    ??? info "2. Start the risotto"
        1. Reduce heat to medium.
        2. In the same pot, melt 1 tbsp butter.
        3. Add the garlic and onion. Cook for 2 minutes until the onion is translucent but not browned.
        4. Add the wine and simmer for about 2 minutes, scraping the base, until mostly evaporated.
        5. Add the arborio rice and stir for 1 minute until semi-translucent.

    ??? info "3. Cook the rice"
        1. Add about 1 1/2 cups warm stock. Stir, then leave for about 3 minutes, stirring a few times, until mostly absorbed.
        2. Add another 1 1/2 cups stock and repeat.
        3. Repeat until all the stock is used and the rice is just cooked but still creamy and a little loose.

    ??? info "4. Finish"
        1. Add the cream and optional butter. Stir vigorously.
        2. Stir in the parmesan, salt, and pepper.
        3. Stir through half the mushrooms.
        4. Taste and adjust seasoning if needed.
        5. Reheat the remaining mushrooms if needed.

    ??? info "5. Serve"
        1. Spoon the risotto into bowls.
        2. Top with the reserved mushrooms and any buttery juices from the bowl.
        3. Finish with parsley or chives and extra parmesan.
        4. Serve immediately.

??? note "Notes"
    - White / button mushrooms or Swiss brown / cremini both work well.
    - A mix of sliced and quartered mushrooms gives better texture.
    - If avoiding wine, replace it with more chicken stock.
    - Cream and extra butter are optional; cream is the better choice if using only one.
    - If the rice is still too firm after the stock is used, add hot water 1/2 cup at a time until done.
    - The finished risotto should be creamy and loose, not stiff or stodgy.
    - Leftovers keep 3–4 days in the fridge. Reheat with a splash of milk.

??? note "Serving ideas"
    - Serve as a main with a crisp green salad.
    - Or serve smaller portions as a starter.
    - Good with chicken, fish, prawns, roast pork, roast beef, or lamb.
