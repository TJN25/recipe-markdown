# Moroccan Lamb Stew

??? note "Ingredients"

    2 servings

    ??? info "For the Stew Base"
        - 1 tbsp olive oil or canola oil (cooking fat)
        - 1 onion, diced (aromatic base)
        - 2 carrots, cubed (sweetness, texture)
        - 3 small agria potatoes, peeled and cubed (hearty texture, thickener)
        - 250g lamb, cubed (protein)
        - 2 garlic cloves, minced (aromatic flavor)
        - 150g daikon radish, peeled and cubed (texture, mild peppery flavor)
        - 100g frozen green beans, cut into 2cm pieces (texture, freshness)
        - 3 plum tomatoes from a can, halved (acidity, umami)
        - 1/2 tbsp tomato paste (concentrated flavor, thickener)
        - 1 beef stock cube dissolved in 1.5 cups hot water (depth of flavor)
        - 1/2 tsp ground cinnamon (warm spice)
        - 1/2 tsp ground allspice (warm, complex flavor)
        - 1 bay leaf (aromatic)
        - 2 spring onions, sliced (fresh finish, color)

    ??? info "Spice Blend"
        - 1/2 tsp smoked paprika (smoky depth)
        - 1/2 tsp ground cumin (earthy warmth)
        - 1/2 tsp ground coriander (citrusy notes)
        - 1/4 tsp turmeric (color, earthiness)
        - 1/4 tsp cayenne pepper (heat, adjust to taste)
        - 1/4 tsp ground black pepper (heat)
        - 1/2 tsp salt (seasoning)
        - 1 tbsp honey (balances acidity)
        - Juice of 1/4 lemon (brightness)
        Preparation Instructions
        Prep Stage (10 minutes):
        1. Dice onion and set aside in a small bowl.
        2. Cube carrots and potatoes, place in a medium bowl.
        3. Mince garlic and set aside in a ramekin.
        4. Peel and cube daikon into 2cm pieces, place in a separate bowl.
        5. Measure frozen green beans and set aside.
        6. Slice spring onions, separating white and green parts.
        7. Measure all spices into a small bowl and mix well.
        8. Dissolve beef stock cube in 1.5 cups hot water.
        Cooking Stage (2 hours):
        1. Heat oil in the 24cm cast iron pan over medium heat.
        2. Add onions, carrots, and potatoes. Sauté for 4-5 minutes until onions begin to soften.
        3. Add garlic and white parts of spring onions, cook for 30 seconds until fragrant.
        4. Add cubed lamb and brown on all sides (8-10 minutes).
        5. Add spice blend, ground allspice, and stir to coat the meat and vegetables, cooking for 1 minute to bloom the spices.
        6. Add tomato paste and stir for 1 minute.
        7. Add plum tomatoes and beef stock, add bay leaf and ground cinnamon.
        8. Bring to a simmer, then reduce heat to low, cover, and simmer for 1.5 hours, stirring occasionally. Check liquid levels periodically and add a little more water if needed.
        9. Add daikon and continue to simmer for 20 minutes.
        10. Add frozen green beans and simmer for another 15 minutes until tender and lamb is fork-tender.
        11. Stir in honey and lemon juice.
        12. Add green parts of spring onions.
        13. Taste and adjust seasoning as needed.

??? note "Serving Suggestions"
    - Serve hot over Moroccan couscous with baby spinach, mint and lemon zest mixed through.
    - Add a dollop of hummus on the side.
    - Garnish with fresh mint leaves from your garden.

??? note "Notes"
    - The baby spinach will be incorporated into the couscous rather than the stew.
    - Spring onions are added in two stages: white parts early for flavor, green parts at the end for freshness and color.
    - Frozen green beans can go directly into the stew without thawing.
    - For cubed lamb, the longer cooking time (about 2 hours total) is necessary to ensure the meat becomes tender.
    - If you want to eat at 7:30pm, start cooking at 5:00pm to allow enough time for the lamb to become tender.
    - The allspice adds a wonderful warm, complex flavor that works perfectly in Moroccan cuisine.
    - While sumac isn't used in this stew, it would be excellent sprinkled over the couscous or used in other Middle Eastern dishes.
