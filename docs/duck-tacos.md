# Duck Tacos with Red Chili Sauce

**Makes:** 4-6 tacos (2 servings)

??? note "Ingredients"

    ??? info "Core Components"
        - Leftover duck meat
        - Soft corn tortillas (flour works but corn is better with duck)
        - Red chili sauce (as per previous recipe)

    ??? info "Additional Toppings"
        - Fresh coriander/cilantro
        - Lime juice
        - Your existing pickled daikon and carrot
        - Spring onions, finely sliced
        - Optional: diced fresh chili for extra heat

    ??? info "Prep Steps"
        2. Duck Prep:
        - Shred duck meat into bite-sized pieces
        - Heat cast iron pan until very hot
        - Quickly sear meat to crisp edges (30-60 seconds)
        3. Tortilla Prep:
        - Warm in cast iron pan after duck
        - Keep warm wrapped in clean kitchen towel
        Assembly Order (bottom to top):
        1. Warm tortilla
        2. Light drizzle of sauce
        3. Crispy duck meat
        4. Another small drizzle of sauce
        5. Pickled onions
        6. Pickled daikon/carrot
        7. Fresh spring onions
        8. Fresh coriander
        9. Final tiny drizzle of sauce
        10. Serve with lime wedges

??? note "Notes"
    - Double-stack tortillas if they're thin
    - Serve extra sauce on the side
    - Don't oversauce initially - let people add more
    - The pickled elements help cut through duck's richness
    - Garnish plate with extra lime wedges and coriander

    ??? info "Pro Tips"
        - Warm plates before serving
        - Have all components ready before assembly
        - Eat immediately while tortillas are warm and duck is crispy
        - If duck is dry, add a tiny bit of oil when reheating
