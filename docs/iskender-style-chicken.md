# Iskender-Style Chicken

??? note "Ingredients"
    - 2 chicken breasts (about 300-350g total) (protein base)
    - 2 tablespoons olive oil (cooking medium, flavor)
    - 1 teaspoon smoked paprika (depth, color)
    - 1/2 teaspoon cumin (warmth, earthiness)
    - 1/2 teaspoon ground coriander (citrusy notes)
    - 1/4 teaspoon cayenne pepper (heat, optional)
    - 1/2 cup taco sauce (tomato component)
    - 3 tablespoons sour cream (creamy tang, traditional element)
    - Salt and pepper to taste (seasoning)
    - 3g was used (1% by weight of chickennnn)
    - Optional: fresh parsley for garnish (color, freshness)

??? note "Preparation"

    40 minutes total
    Prep Stage (15 minutes)
    - Prepare all components first:
    - Slice chicken breasts into thin strips
    - Measure all spices into small bowls or ramekins
    - Prepare chicken marinade:
    - In a bowl, mix olive oil, smoked paprika, cumin, coriander, cayenne, salt and pepper
    - Toss chicken strips in the spice mixture to coat evenly
    Cooking Stage (25 minutes)
    - Cook chicken:
    - Heat cast iron pan over medium-high heat
    - Cook chicken strips for 5-7 minutes until golden and cooked through (75°C)
    - Remove and keep warm

        - Consider adding sumac (common in Middle Eastern cooking) for authentic citrusy notes
        - A small side of pickled vegetables would add brightness and cut through the richness

        Main option: Pat dry after marinating
        1. Marinate chicken (1% salt by weight)
        2. After marinating, remove chicken pieces from the marinade
        3. Pat each piece thoroughly dry with paper towels
        4. Use minimal fresh oil in the pan (just enough to prevent sticking)

    ??? info "This approach gives you the best of both worlds"
        - The flavor and tenderizing benefits of the marinade
        - A dry surface that can properly brown
        Alternative

    ??? info "If you want to experiment"
        1. Dry spice rub method: Skip the oil in the marinade entirely. Apply salt first (for 15-30 minutes), then pat dry and apply dry spices just before cooking.
        2. Two-stage cooking: For extra-juicy results with good browning:
        - Cook at medium heat until 80% done
        - Remove and pat dry
        - Return to very high heat with fresh oil for final sear
        Additional Notes
        - Make sure your pan is properly preheated before adding any oil
        - A drop of water should dance and evaporate immediately when the pan is ready
        - Use cast iron, not stainless steel
