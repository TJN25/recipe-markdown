    **Serves:** 1

    ??? info "Ingredients"

        **Core:**
        - 120 g pulled mojo pork (protein, fat)
        - 40 g white onion, thin-sliced (sweetness, texture)
        - 125 g cabbage, thin-sliced (bulk, texture, sweetness)
        - 2 dried bird's eye chillies, crushed (heat, flavor)
        - 1 tbsp fried shallots from packet (texture, umami)
        - 1 egg (optional) (richness, protein)

        **Gochujang Sauce:**
        - 1 tsp gochujang (umami, heat, fermented depth)
        - 1 tsp soy sauce (salty, umami)
        - 1/2 tsp fish sauce (umami, salty)
        - 1 tbsp water (helps sauce coat)
        - 1 tsp lime juice (acid, brightness)

        **For Adjustment:**
        - MSG (umami booster)
        - Fish sauce or soy sauce (salty, umami)
        - Kecap manis (sweetness, viscosity)
        - Vinegar (acid)

    ??? info "Preparation"

        1. Slice onion thinly. Slice cabbage thinly. Crush dried chillies.
        2. Mix gochujang, soy sauce, fish sauce, water, and lime juice in a ramekin. Set aside.
        3. Have fried shallots ready in a small bowl.
        4. If using egg, fry separately and keep warm.

    ??? info "Cooking Instructions"

        1. Heat large nonstick fry pan over high heat. Add pork and crisp for 1–2 minutes without stirring. Toss and cook for 30–60 seconds. Remove to a bowl.
        2. Return pan to high heat. Add onion and dried chillies. Stir-fry for 60–90 seconds until onion softens.
        3. Add cabbage and toss for 30 seconds. Cover and cook for 45–60 seconds. Remove lid and continue cooking for 1–2 minutes until cabbage is tender and moisture has evaporated.
        4. Return pork to pan. Reduce heat to medium. Add sauce and toss for 20–40 seconds until everything is coated and glossy.
        5. Remove from heat. Taste and adjust with MSG, fish sauce, soy sauce, kecap manis, or vinegar as needed.
        6. Transfer to serving plate. Top with fried shallots and fried egg if using.

    ??? info "Suggestions for Enhancement"

        - Toast dried chillies briefly in the dry pan before adding other ingredients for deeper flavor.
        - Add a small amount of neutral oil if pork is lean and sticking occurs.
        - Use fresh lime juice at the end instead of in the sauce for brighter acidity.
        - Increase gochujang to 1 1/2 tsp for more pronounced fermented flavor.

    ??? info "Notes on Techniques, Ingredients, and Plating"

        - Ensure cabbage is sliced thinly and uniformly for even cooking.
        - The covering step creates steam to soften cabbage quickly while maintaining texture.
        - Fried shallots lose their crunch when heated, so add only at the end.
        - For plating, place egg on top of the stir-fry with yolk intact. Arrange fried shallots around the edges for visual contrast. Serve with lime wedge on the side.
