
# Peruvian-Style Grilled Chicken with Green Sauce

**Serves:** 2

??? note "Ingredients"

    ??? info "For the Green Sauce"

        - 1.5 jalapeño chiles, roughly chopped *(heat, fruity spice)*
        - 1/2 cup fresh coriander leaves *(freshness, herbaceous)*
        - 1 medium clove garlic *(pungent flavor)*
        - 1/4 cup mayonnaise *(creamy fat base)*
        - 2 tablespoons sour cream *(tangy richness)*
        - 1 teaspoon fresh lime juice *(bright acidity)*
        - 1/2 teaspoon white vinegar *(sharp acidity)*
        - 1 tablespoon extra-virgin olive oil *(smooth fat)*
        - Salt and pepper *(seasoning)*

    ??? info "For Ají Amarillo Substitute"

        - 1/2 habanero, deseeded *(intense heat)*
        - 1/4 red capsicum *(sweetness, body)*
        - 1/4 teaspoon salt *(draws out moisture)*
        - 1 teaspoon neutral oil *(blending aid)*

    ??? info "For the Chicken"

        - 4 bone-in chicken thighs *(protein, fat, flavor)*
        - 2 teaspoons kosher salt *(seasoning, moisture retention)*
        - 1 tablespoon ground cumin *(earthy warmth)*
        - 1 tablespoon paprika *(color, mild pepper flavor)*
        - 1/2 teaspoon black pepper *(heat, aroma)*
        - 1.5 medium cloves garlic, minced *(aromatic base)*
        - 1 tablespoon white vinegar *(tenderizer, tang)*
        - 1 tablespoon vegetable oil *(cooking fat)*

??? note "Method"

    ??? info "Ají Amarillo Substitute"

        1. Blend habanero, capsicum, salt, and oil in blender until smooth paste forms

        2. Use 1/2 tablespoon of this paste in the green sauce

    ??? info "Green Sauce"

        1. Combine jalapeños, ají substitute, coriander, garlic, mayonnaise, sour cream, lime juice, and vinegar in blender

        2. Blend until smooth

        3. With blender running, drizzle in olive oil

        4. Season with salt and pepper

        5. Refrigerate until needed

    ??? info "Chicken"

        1. Pat thighs dry with paper towels

        2. Mix salt, cumin, paprika, pepper, garlic, vinegar, and oil in ramekin until paste forms

        3. Rub all over chicken thighs, including under skin

        4. Heat cast iron pan over medium-high heat

        5. Sear thighs skin-side down 4-5 minutes until crispy

        6. Flip and transfer pan to toaster oven at 200°C

        7. Cook until internal temperature reaches 75°C

        8. Rest 5 minutes before serving

??? note "Notes"

    ??? info "Suggestions for Enhancement"

        - Make ají substitute in larger batch and freeze in ice cube trays
        - Score chicken skin in crosshatch pattern for extra crispiness

    ??? info "Notes on Techniques, Ingredients, and Plating"

        - **Habanero Handling:** Wear gloves and avoid touching face. Start with less and add more to taste.
        - **Thigh Cooking:** Bone-in thighs take longer than breast meat - use thermometer to check doneness.
        - **Plating:** Serve thighs with generous dollops of green sauce and lime wedges.
