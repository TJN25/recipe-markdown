
# Creamy Chicken Soup

??? note "Ingredients"

    ??? info "Base Vegetables"

        - 2 medium carrots (about 150g), diced *(sweetness, texture)*
        - 1 large onion (about 200g), diced *(savory foundation)*
        - 4 cloves garlic, minced *(aromatic depth)*

    ??? info "Protein & Starch"

        - 500g chicken breast, diced into 2cm pieces *(protein, substance)*
        - 300g potatoes, chopped into bite-sized pieces *(starch, heartiness)*

    ??? info "Chicken Seasoning (for pre-seasoning)"

        - 1 tsp salt *(tenderizing, flavor penetration)*
        - ½ tsp garlic powder *(flavor infusion)*
        - ½ tsp paprika *(color, mild warmth)*
        - ¼ tsp black pepper *(heat, aroma)*

    ??? info "Soup Seasonings"

        - ½ tsp garlic powder *(concentrated garlic flavor)*
        - 1 tsp onion powder *(umami depth)*
        - ½ tsp paprika *(mild warmth, color)*
        - 1 tsp celery seeds *(concentrated celery flavor, aromatic)*
        - ½ tsp salt *(flavor enhancement)*
        - Fresh cracked pepper *(heat, aroma)*
        - 1 bay leaf *(herbal complexity)*
        - ½ tsp dried thyme *(earthy herb notes)*
        - ½ tsp dried oregano *(Mediterranean warmth)*
        - ¼ tsp cayenne pepper *(gentle heat)*

    ??? info "Liquid Base"

        - 1 litre chicken stock *(savory liquid foundation)*

    ??? info "Creaminess (added after initial cooking)"

        - 180ml cream *(richness, silky texture)*
        - 60g cream cheese *(tangy creaminess, body)*
        - 1½ tbsp cornflour *(thickening agent)*
        - 2 tbsp fresh parsley *(fresh herb finish, color)*

??? note "Preparation"

    ??? info "Prep Stage (15 minutes)"

        1. Dice chicken breast into 2cm pieces

        2. In a medium bowl, toss diced chicken with chicken seasoning ingredients

        3. Let chicken sit for 10-15 minutes to absorb seasoning *(salt draws out moisture then reabsorbs for tenderness)*

        4. Dice carrots and onion into 1cm pieces

        5. Mince garlic using microplane or knife

        6. Chop potatoes into 2cm pieces (leave skin on for texture and nutrients)

        7. Lightly crush celery seeds using pestle and mortar to release oils *(enhances flavor release)*

??? note "Method"

    ??? info "Initial Cooking"

        1. Add seasoned chicken pieces, vegetables, potatoes, and all soup seasonings (including crushed celery seeds) to slow cooker

        2. Pour chicken stock over ingredients

        3. Cook on HIGH for 2½ hours or LOW for 5 hours *(reduced time due to smaller chicken pieces)*

    ??? info "Finishing Stage"

        1. In a small bowl, mix cornflour with 2 tbsp water to create slurry

        2. Stir in cream, cream cheese, and cornflour slurry

        3. Cook on HIGH for additional 30 minutes

        4. Remove bay leaf, stir in fresh parsley

        5. Taste and adjust seasoning with salt and pepper

??? note "Notes"

    ??? info "Enhancement Suggestions"

        #### Pre-seasoning Benefits

        - Salt breaks down proteins for more tender chicken
        - Seasoning penetrates meat rather than just coating surface
        - Prevents chicken from becoming tough during long cooking
        - Creates more evenly flavored protein throughout

        #### Advanced Pre-seasoning Technique

        - Add 1 tbsp Greek yogurt to chicken seasoning mix for extra tenderness *(acid helps break down proteins)*
        - Let chicken marinate for up to 2 hours in refrigerator for deeper flavor
        - Pat chicken dry before adding to slow cooker if using yogurt marinade

        #### Other Improvements

        - Lightly brown seasoned chicken pieces in cast iron pan before slow cooking for enhanced flavor
        - Sauté vegetables in olive oil for 5 minutes before adding to slow cooker
        - Add splash of white wine (60ml) with vegetables for acidity balance

    ??? info "Storage & Serving"

        - **Portions:** Makes 4-6 servings (approximately 400ml each)
        - **Storage:** Refrigerate in airtight containers for up to 4 days
        - **Note:** Pre-seasoned chicken maintains better texture when reheated

    ??? info "Notes on Techniques, Ingredients, and Plating"

        #### Pre-seasoning Science

        - Salt draws moisture from chicken initially, then proteins break down and reabsorb liquid
        - This process creates more tender, flavorful meat that won't dry out
        - Smaller pieces cook more evenly and absorb flavors better
        - Pre-seasoning prevents the "boiled chicken" texture common in slow cooker recipes

        #### Timing Adjustments

        - Diced chicken cooks faster than whole breasts, reducing overall cooking time
        - Check doneness at 2 hours on HIGH to prevent overcooking
        - Chicken should reach 75°C internal temperature

        #### Alternative Pre-seasoning Blends

        - Mediterranean: oregano, thyme, garlic powder, lemon zest
        - Indian-inspired: garam masala, turmeric, ginger powder
        - Smoky: smoked paprika, cumin, chipotle powder

        #### Plating Presentation

        - Garnish with extra cracked pepper and fresh herbs
        - Drizzle quality olive oil for richness
        - Serve with crusty bread or herb butter toasties
