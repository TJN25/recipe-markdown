# Teriyaki Drizzle

**Makes:** ~⅓ cup, enough for 2-3 bowls

??? note "Ingredients"

    ??? info "Sauce Base"

        - 1.5 tablespoons soy sauce (salty, umami - provides savory backbone)
        - 1.5 tablespoons kecap manis (sweet, umami - adds molasses complexity and body)
        - 1 tablespoon mirin (sweet, umami - adds gentle sweetness and depth)
        - 2 tablespoons brown sugar (sweet - contributes molasses notes and caramelized flavor)
        - 1cm ginger (pungent, warming - adds spice and aromatic depth)
        - 1 tsp minced garlic (pungent, savory - adds aromatic complexity)

??? note "Method"

    ??? info "Prep (2 minutes)"

        1. Measure all ingredients and have them ready near the stovetop.

    ??? info "Cooking (5 minutes)"

        1. Add everything. Whisk together until combined.

        2. Place saucepan over medium heat. Whisk constantly as it heats.

        3. Once the mixture begins to bubble and simmer (you'll see bubbles breaking the surface), reduce heat to low.

        4. Continue simmering for 2-3 minutes, stirring frequently with a spoon or whisk, until sauce thickens to a syrupy consistency that coats the back of a spoon. Draw a line through the sauce on the spoon with your finger - if it holds, it's ready.

        5. Remove from heat. The sauce will thicken slightly more as it cools to room temperature.

??? note "Notes"

    ??? info "Suggestions for Enhancement"

        - **Consistency adjustment:** If sauce becomes too thick as it cools, whisk in water 1 teaspoon at a time to thin. If too thin, return to heat and simmer longer, or mix ½ teaspoon cornstarch with 1 tablespoon cold water and whisk in, then simmer for 1 minute more.
        - **Basting option:** Reserve 2 tablespoons of the finished sauce and brush onto chicken in the last 2 minutes of grilling for a light glaze. Use the remaining sauce for drizzling over the assembled bowl.

??? note "Storage"

    - Store in an airtight container or squeeze bottle in the refrigerator for up to 2 weeks.
    - Sauce will thicken when cold. Gently rewarm in the very small saucepan over low heat, whisking constantly, or microwave for 10-15 seconds and stir. Add a splash of water if needed to return to drizzling consistency.
    - Do not freeze (cornstarch-thickened sauces can separate and become grainy when thawed).
