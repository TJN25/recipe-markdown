
# Chilli Con Carne (Dutch Oven)

**Serves:** 4-5 

??? note "Ingredients"

    ??? info "Aromatics"

        - 1 tbsp olive oil (fat, cooking medium)
        - 3 garlic cloves, minced (flavor, aroma)
        - 1 onion, diced (sweetness, body)
        - 1 red capsicum, diced (sweetness, texture)

    ??? info "Protein"

        - 500g beef mince (protein, umami, body)

    ??? info "Tomato Base"

        - 3 tbsp tomato paste (umami, thickening, depth)
        - 800g can crushed tomatoes (acidity, body, liquid)

    ??? info "Bulk"

        - ½ cup frozen corn

    ??? info "Seasoning"

        - 2 beef stock cubes, crumbled (umami, saltiness)
        - 1½ tsp sugar (balances acidity)
        - 1-2 tsp cayenne pepper (heat - use 1 tsp for mild warmth, 1.5 tsp for medium, 2 tsp for proper heat)
        - 4 tsp paprika (color, mild sweetness)
        - 5 tsp cumin (earthy, warm)
        - 2 tsp garlic powder (depth)
        - 2 tsp onion powder (depth)
        - 2 tsp oregano (herbal, aromatic)
        - 1½ tsp salt

    ??? info "Liquid"

        - 1½ cups (375ml) water (for slow simmer in Dutch oven)

    ??? info "Serving"

        - Rice, corn chips
        - Sour cream, yoghurt
        - Cheese
        - Limes (for squeezing juice)
        - Fresh coriander
        - Avocado

??? note "Method"

    ??? info "Prep"

        - Mince garlic
        - Dice onion and capsicum into ~1cm pieces
        - Drain corn
        - Combine all spices in a small bowl

    ??? info "Cooking"

        1. Heat Dutch oven over medium-high heat. Add oil.

        2. Cook onion until soft, add garlic and cook until fragrant.

        3. Add capsicum, cook 5 minutes until onion is translucent.

        4. Turn heat to high. Add beef mince, breaking it up as you go. Cook until mostly browned.

        5. Add entire spice mix. Continue cooking, stirring frequently, until beef is completely browned and spices are fragrant (this blooms the spices and develops flavor).

        6. Add tomato paste, crushed tomatoes, corn, stock cubes, sugar, msg, bay leaf, and 1½ cups water. Stir to combine.

        7. Bring to a simmer, then reduce heat to low. Cover with lid slightly ajar.

        8. Cook for 1½-2 hours, stirring occasionally. Chilli should bubble very gently. The beef will become more tender and sauce will become richer and more concentrated. If it becomes too thick, add water ¼ cup at a time.

        9. Taste and adjust salt before serving.

??? note "Notes"

    ??? info "Serving Options"

        - Over rice with toppings
        - In bowls with corn chips or warm tortillas for dunking
        - With cornbread or cornbread muffins
        - Chilli dogs (makes ~20)
        - With flatbread

    ??? info "Toppings"

        - Sour cream or Greek yogurt
        - Grated cheese
        - Fresh coriander/cilantro
        - Sliced spring onions or diced red onion
        - Diced avocado
        - Fresh jalapeño or other chillies
        - Lime wedges

    ??? info "Slow Cooker Adaptation"

        Complete steps 1-5 using only ¼ cup water, transfer to slow cooker, cook 6-8 hours on low. Add corn in final hour to prevent mushiness.

    ??? info "Storage"

        Improves overnight in fridge. Freezes excellently for 3+ months.
