# Spiced Chicken Bao Buns with Sriracha Mayo

??? note "Ingredients"

    ??? info "For the chicken"
        - 300g chicken thigh (protein, main component)
        - 1 Tbsp soy sauce (umami, salt)
        - 1 tsp sesame oil (aromatic, nutty depth)
        - 1 tsp garlic powder or minced garlic (flavor depth)
        - 1 tsp ground ginger or ginger paste (warmth, aroma)
        - ½ tsp Sichuan pepper, ground (numbing heat, complexity)
        - ¼ tsp allspice (warm, complex spice notes)
        - ½ tsp smoked paprika (smoky depth, color)
        - 1 dried bird's eye chili, crushed (heat, intensity)
        - 1 egg (binding agent)
        - 3 Tbsp cornflour (crispy coating)
        - 3 Tbsp canola oil (for frying)

    ??? info "For the sriracha mayo"
        - 3 Tbsp Kewpie mayo (creamy base)
        - 1-2 tsp sriracha sauce (heat, to taste)
        - ½ tsp honey (balance, sweetness)
        - Few drops of lemon juice (brightness)
        - ¼ tsp sesame oil (aromatic finish)

    ??? info "For assembly"
        - Bao buns (vessel)
        - Shredded cabbage (crunch, freshness)
        - Grated carrot (sweetness, color)
        - ½ cucumber, thinly sliced (coolness, crunch)
        - Sliced spring onion (sharp bite, garnish)
        - 2 Tbsp roasted peanuts, crushed (texture, nutty flavor)

??? note "Preparation"
    Prep Stage (15 minutes)
    1. Slice chicken thighs into 1cm pieces and place in a medium bowl.
    2. Crush the dried bird's eye chili using a mortar and pestle or the flat side of your knife.
    3. In a small ramekin, mix soy sauce, sesame oil, garlic powder, ginger, Sichuan tpepper, allspice, smoked paprika, and crushed chili.
    4. Pour spice mixture over chicken and mix thoroughly to coat.
    5. Add egg and cornflour to the chicken, mix well. Marinate in the fridge for 15-20 minutes.
    6. While chicken marinates, prepare the coleslaw by combining shredded cabbage and grated carrot in a bowl.
    7. Thinly slice cucumber into half-moons or matchsticks.
    8. Crush roasted peanuts using a mortar and pestle or by placing them in a bag and crushing with a rolling pin.
    9. Make sriracha mayo by mixing Kewpie mayo, sriracha sauce, honey, a few drops of lemon juice, and a small drop of sesame oil. Adjust heat level to preference.
    10. Slice spring onions thinly and set aside for garnish.
    Cooking Stage (15 minutes)
    1. Heat canola oil in your wok over medium-high heat.
    2. Test oil temperature by dropping a small piece of batter in - it should sizzle immediately.
    3. Fry chicken pieces in batches (don't overcrowd) for 2-3 minutes until golden brown and crispy. The wok's curved sides will help contain splatter while allowing easy access for turning the chicken.
    4. Transfer cooked chicken to a paper towel-lined plate to drain excess oil.
    5. While cooking the last batch, prepare to steam bao buns according to package instructions (typically 3-4 minutes).
    Assembly (5 minutes)
    1. Open steamed bao buns and spread a small amount of sriracha mayo on the inside.
    2. Add a layer of coleslaw to the bottom.
    3. Place a few slices of cucumber on top of the coleslaw.
    4. Add 3-4 pieces of crispy chicken.
    5. Drizzle with additional sriracha mayo.
    6. Sprinkle with crushed peanuts and sliced spring onions.
    7. Serve immediately while chicken is still crispy.
    Enhancements
    Technique Improvements
    - Using a wok for frying gives you better temperature control and requires less oil than a flat pan.
    - For the cucumber: After slicing, lightly salt the cucumber slices and let sit for 5 minutes, then pat dry. This removes excess moisture and intensifies the flavor.
    - For extra crunch in the peanuts: Toast them briefly in a dry pan before crushing to enhance their flavor.
    Notes on Culinary Techniques
    - The combination of cool cucumber, spicy chicken, and crunchy peanuts creates a perfect balance of temperature, texture, and flavor.
    - Bird's eye chili provides a clean, bright heat that complements the numbing quality of Sichuan pepper.
    - The crushed peanuts add not only texture but also a rich, roasted flavor that enhances the overall umami profile of the dish.
    - For best results, assemble each bao just before eating to maintain the contrast between hot chicken and cool vegetables.
