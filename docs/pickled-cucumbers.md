# Quick Pickled Cucumbers

??? note "Ingredients"
    - 90g cucumber (what you have)
    - 60ml white vinegar (acidity/preservative)
    - 1 tbsp raw sugar (balances acidity)
    - 1 tsp salt (draws out moisture/preservative)
    - ¼ tsp mustard powder (flavor complexity)
    - ¼ tsp dill (classic pickle flavor)
    - Optional: ¼ tsp red chili flakes or a small piece of fresh cayenne chili (heat)

??? note "Preparation"

    10 minutes
    1. Wash cucumber thoroughly and pat dry with paper towel
    2. Slice cucumber thinly (2-3mm) - a mandoline would be ideal for consistent, thin slices if you have one
    3. Place cucumber slices in colander, sprinkle with salt, and let sit for 5 minutes (draws out excess moisture)
    4. Meanwhile, in small saucepan, combine vinegar, sugar, mustard powder, and dill
    5. Heat mixture on stovetop until sugar dissolves (about 2 minutes), then remove from heat
    6. Rinse salt from cucumbers and pat dry with paper towel
    7. Pack cucumber slices into airtight container
    8. Pour vinegar mixture over cucumbers, ensuring they're fully submerged
    9. Seal container and let cool to room temperature
    10. Refrigerate for at least 1 hour before serving (best after 24 hours)

??? note "Storage"
    - Keep refrigerated in airtight container for up to 2 weeks

??? note "Serving Suggestions"
    - Perfect side for sandwiches with your Mexican chicken
    - Add to burgers using your bread buns
    - Chop and mix with Greek yogurt for a quick tzatziki-style sauce
    - Add to tacos with your tortillas and Mexican chicken

??? note "Enhancement Tips"
    - For extra crunch: Add 1 tsp of mustard seeds instead of powder
    - For complexity: Add ¼ tsp of toasted cumin seeds
    - For brightness: Add a small strip of lemon zest to the brine
    Technique Notes
    - Garlic is optional and not necessary for a good pickle
    - A mandoline will give you perfectly even, thin slices which pickle more consistently
    - If you don't have a mandoline, use your chef knife and try to keep slices as even as possible
    - The thinner you slice, the faster they'll pickle and the more intense the flavor will be
