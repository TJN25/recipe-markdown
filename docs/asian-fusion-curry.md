# Asian-fusion Curry Style Chicken

??? note "Ingredients"

    ??? info "Rub"
        - 0.5 tsp hot chilli powder/bird eye chillies soaked and finely chopped
        - 0.5 tsp ground turmeric
        - 0.5 tsp ground cumin
        - 1.5g salt
        - 150g chicken breast (large slices)
        - 10g fresh coriander

    ??? info "Rice"
        - ⅓ cup basmati rice
        - 100g tinned coconut milk

    ??? info "Sauce ingredients to replace "thai paste""
        - **gochujang 1 tbsp**
        - **fish sauce 1–2 tsp**
        - **bird-eye chilli** (to heat tolerance)
        - **vinegar 1 tsp** (white wine vinegar or cider vinegar) 
        - 
          - Rice vinegar is fine too (milder); use **2 tsp** if you want similar “lift”
        - **MSG** pinch (optional)
        - **soy** small dash (optional to taste)
        - ginger ¼ tsp
        - garlic ½ tsp minced

    ??? info "Remaining ingredients for sauce"
        - 0.5 tsp cider vinegar (consider juice of half a lime, add very late)
        - 0.5 tbsp honey
        - Salt and pepper to taste

??? note "Method"

    ??? info "Preheat"
        Preheat the oven to 230°C and line a large baking tray with baking paper.

    ??? info "Assemble the marinade"
        Add chilli powder (or bird eye chilli), turmeric, cumin, and salt to a large mixing bowl and mix.

    ??? info "Mix and coat"
        Add the chicken to the bowl and give everything a good mix, ensuring the chicken is well coated.

    ??? info "Tray them up"
        Lay the coated chicken onto the lined tray, ensuring they are well-spaced. Let rest while preparing other things (or do this earlier in the day and place in fridge covered).

    ??? info "Roast"
        Roast in the oven for 12-14 minutes, until charred in places and cooked throughout. Get on with the rest of the dish in the next steps while you wait.

    ??? info "Cook your rice"
        Finely chop coriander and set aside. Tip rice into a medium saucepan along with 50g of coconut milk, and water topped up to meet the recommended amount on the packet and a generous pinch of salt. Put the pan over a high heat, bring to a boil, then reduce the heat to low. Cover the pan with a tight-fitting lid and simmer for 10-12 minutes (might need longer according to the packet), until the liquid has been absorbed by the rice and the grains are soft with a very slight bite.

    ??? info "Make the sauce"
        Heat a small saucepan over medium heat. Add the remaining 50g of coconut milk and the sauce ingredients, then whisk to combine. Once smooth, (taste to check if it needs vinegar and honey) add cider vinegar, honey and bring to a simmer. Season with salt (careful here as everything else has salt) and pepper to taste.

    ??? info "Fluff the rice"
        Once the timer for the rice has gone off, fluff the rice with a fork and fold in half of the chopped coriander.

    ??? info "Toss"
        Remove the chicken from the oven, pour over the curry sauce and toss to coat evenly in the sauce.

    ??? info "Serve"
        Divide the rice between plates and top with the chicken slices. Garnish with the rest of the coriander.
