
??? note "One Pan Cheesy French Onion Chicken with Vegetables (2 Servings)"

    ??? info "Ingredients:"

        **For the Chicken:**
        - 2 chicken breasts (300g each), diced into 2cm pieces - *protein base, faster cooking*
        - 1 tbsp olive oil - *fat for searing and flavor*
        - 1 large brown onion, thinly sliced - *sweetness and French onion flavor*
        - 2 medium carrots, diced into 1cm pieces - *sweetness and texture*
        - 150g streaky bacon, cut into 1cm strips - *smoky, salty richness*
        - 150ml cream - *creamy richness and sauce base*
        - 125ml (½ cup) chicken stock (from cube) - *umami depth and deglazing*
        - 2 tbsp French onion soup mix (you'll need to buy) - *concentrated French onion flavor*
        - 150g everyday cheese, grated - *melty topping and richness*
        - Spring onion, sliced - *fresh garnish and color*

        **For the Mashed Potato:**
        - 600g potatoes (you'll need to buy) - *starchy base*
        - Remaining cream from above - *richness and smoothness*
        - 2 tbsp butter - *fat and flavor*
        - Salt and pepper - *seasoning*

        **For the Vegetables:**
        - 200g green beans (you'll need to buy), trimmed - *fresh crunch and color*
        - 1 cup frozen corn - *sweetness and texture*
        - 1 tbsp butter - *richness for vegetables*

    ??? info "Preparation Steps:"

        1. **Prep ingredients**: Dice chicken into 2cm pieces, slice onion thinly, dice carrots into 1cm pieces, cut bacon into strips, grate cheese, prepare stock from cube
        2. **Prep vegetables**: Trim green beans, measure frozen corn
        3. **Start potatoes**: Peel and quarter potatoes, place in large saucepan with cold salted water
        4. **Set up**: Have cast iron pan ready for chicken, medium saucepan for vegetables

    ??? info "Cooking Instructions:"

        **Mashed Potato (Start First):**
        1. Bring potatoes to boil in large saucepan, reduce to simmer and cook 15-20 minutes until tender when pierced with knife
        2. Drain thoroughly and return to pan
        3. Mash with potato masher, then gradually add butter and remaining cream (about 50ml) until smooth and creamy
        4. Season with salt and pepper, keep warm with lid on

        **Chicken (Start when potatoes are halfway cooked):**
        1. **Step 1**: Heat olive oil in 24cm cast iron pan over high heat. Add diced chicken in single layer (work in batches if needed) and sear 2-3 minutes until golden. Remove to plate.
        2. **Step 2**: Add sliced onion and diced carrots to same pan, cook 4-5 minutes until onion is softening and carrots are starting to tender. Add bacon strips and cook 3-4 minutes until starting to crisp.
        3. **Step 3**: Pour in chicken stock and scrape up browned bits. Add 100ml cream and French onion soup mix, stir to combine and bring to gentle boil.
        4. **Step 4**: Return chicken to pan, toss to coat in sauce. Sprinkle grated cheese on top and transfer to toaster oven at 200°C for 12-15 minutes until chicken is cooked through (75°C), carrots are tender, and cheese is golden and bubbly.

        **Vegetables (Start 8 minutes before chicken is done):**
        1. Bring salted water to boil in medium saucepan
        2. Add green beans, cook 4-5 minutes until bright green and tender-crisp
        3. Add frozen corn in final 2 minutes to heat through
        4. Drain well, toss with butter, salt and pepper
        5. **Step 5**: Garnish chicken with sliced spring onion.

    ??? info "Serving:"

        Serve chicken directly from cast iron pan alongside generous portions of creamy mashed potato and the buttered beans and corn mixture. The colorful vegetables provide fresh contrast to the rich, cheesy chicken.

    ??? info "Enhancements:"

        - **Herb the vegetables**: Toss beans and corn with fresh parsley or dried thyme
        - **Garlic butter vegetables**: Add minced garlic to the butter for the vegetables
        - **Lemon brightness**: Squeeze lemon juice over the vegetables before serving
        - **Crispy carrots**: Start carrots 2 minutes before onions for better caramelization

    ??? info "Storage:"

        - Refrigerate leftovers up to 3 days
        - Reheat chicken gently in toaster oven, add splash of stock if needed
        - Reheat vegetables quickly in pan with a little butter

    ??? info "Notes on Techniques, Ingredients, and Plating:"

        **Technique**: Adding carrots to the main dish integrates them into the sauce while maintaining some texture. The 1cm dice ensures they cook through in the oven time without becoming mushy.

        **Timing coordination**: Start potatoes first (20 mins), then chicken (15 mins), then vegetables (6-8 mins). Everything should finish together for optimal serving temperature.

        **Plating**: Create a colorful plate with the golden, bubbly chicken as the centerpiece, fluffy white mashed potato on one side, and the vibrant green beans with bright yellow corn on the other. The spring onion garnish ties the colors together beautifully. The variety of textures - creamy sauce, fluffy potato, tender-crisp vegetables - creates an engaging eating experience.
