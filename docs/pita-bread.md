# Pita Bread

??? note "Pita Bread (Cold Ferment Version)"

    **Makes:** 4 pitas

    ??? info "Ingredients"
        - 250g bread flour (structure)
        - 1 tsp active dried yeast (leavening)
        - 1 tsp sugar (feeds yeast)
        - 1/2 tsp salt (flavor, controls yeast)
        - 170ml water at room temp (structure, steam)

    ??? info "Method"
        Morning Prep (20 minutes active)
        1. Mix flour and water for autolyse (20-30 minutes)
        2. Bloom yeast with sugar in 2 tbsp water from total amount
        3. After autolyse, add yeast mixture and salt
        4. Perform 2 sets of stretch & folds, 15 minutes apart
        5. Place in covered container with room to rise
        6. Refrigerate 8-12 hours

        Evening Prep
        1. Remove dough from fridge 1 hour before shaping
        2. Place cast iron pan in oven
        3. Preheat oven to 260°C with fan
        4. Divide dough into 4 pieces (about 105g each)
        5. Shape into balls, rest 10 minutes
        6. Roll into 18-20cm circles
        7. Rest 10 minutes while oven finishes heating

    ??? info "Baking"
        1. Bake one at a time
        2. Transfer to hot pan carefully
        3. Cook 2-3 minutes until puffed
        4. Flip for 30 seconds to set other side
        5. Stack and wrap in clean kitchen towel

??? note "Pita Bread Recipe (Quick)"

    **Makes:** 4 pitas

    ??? info "Ingredients"
        - 250g bread flour (structure)
        - 1 tsp active dried yeast (leavening)
        - 1 tsp sugar (feeds yeast)
        - 1/2 tsp salt (flavor, controls yeast)
        - 170ml warm water (35-38°C) (hydration, activates yeast quickly)

    ??? info "Method"
        **Total:** 3-4 hours

        Initial Prep (30 minutes)
        1. Bloom yeast: In a small ramekin, mix yeast with sugar and 2 tbsp warm water. Let sit until foamy (5-10 minutes).
        2. Mix dough: In a large metal bowl, combine flour and salt. Add bloomed yeast mixture and remaining water. Mix until no dry flour remains.
        3. Knead: Turn onto lightly floured wooden board and knead for 5-8 minutes until smooth and elastic.
        4. First rise: Place dough in a lightly oiled bowl, cover with plastic wrap or damp towel, and let rise in a warm spot for 1-1.5 hours until doubled in size.

        Shaping and Second Rise (45 minutes)
        1. Divide: Gently deflate dough and divide into 4 equal pieces (about 105g each).
        2. Shape: Form each piece into a tight ball by pulling edges to center and pinching closed. Place seam-side down on floured surface.
        3. Rest: Cover with a clean kitchen towel and let rest for 15-20 minutes.
        4. Roll out: Roll each ball into an 18-20cm circle, maintaining even thickness.
        5. Final proof: Let the rolled pitas rest for 15-20 minutes while preheating the oven.

    ??? info "Baking"
        1. Preheat: Place cast iron pan in oven and preheat to 260°C with fan setting.
        2. Bake individually: Transfer one pita at a time to the hot pan.
        3. Watch for puff: Bake for 2-3 minutes until puffed.
        4. Flip: Turn for 30 seconds to set other side.
        5. Keep warm: Stack and wrap in clean kitchen towel.

        Tips for Quick Success
        - Use slightly warmer water than the cold ferment version to speed up yeast activity
        - Place dough in a warm spot for faster rising (near a sunny window or on top of a warm appliance)
        - If your kitchen is cool, preheat your oven to its lowest setting for 1-2 minutes, then turn it off and use as a proofing box
        - The pitas won't have quite the same depth of flavor as the cold ferment version, but will still be delicious and fresh

        This quick version will give you fresh pita bread within 3-4 hours while maintaining good texture and flavor.
