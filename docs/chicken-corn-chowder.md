# Chicken Corn Chowder (User Adjusted & Revised Method)

**Makes:** Approx. 4-5 servings
**Prep:** ~20 mins
**Cook:** ~1 hour (includes simmering for flavour development)

??? note "Ingredients"
    - 4 strips Uncooked Bacon, cut into pieces (Fat for cooking, flavor base, saltiness)
    - 30g Unsalted Butter (Fat for cooking, flavor, richness)
    - 1 Medium Yellow Onion, diced (Aromatic base, sweetness)
    - 2 Medium Carrots, diced (Sweetness, texture, color)
    - 2 tsp Diced Jalapeño (Flavor, heat)
    - 1 (approx. 90-100g) Roasted Red Pepper (Capsicum), diced (Flavor, sweetness, color, texture)
    - 4-5 cloves Garlic, minced (Aromatic, depth of flavor)
    - ~2.5 Tbsp (20g) All-purpose Flour (Thickener - forms roux)
    - 3-4g Kosher Salt (+ more to taste & for seasoning chicken) (Seasoning, enhances other flavors)
    - 1/2 tsp Ground Black Pepper (+ more to taste & for seasoning chicken) (Spice, warmth)
    - 1/2 tsp Smoked Paprika (Smokiness, color, mild sweetness)
    - 1/2 tsp Dry Ground Mustard (Mustard Powder) (Tanginess, depth)
    - Optional: 1 Bay Leaf (Subtle aromatic depth)
    - 1 1/4 cups (~300ml) Concentrated Chicken Broth (Liquid base, savory flavor)
    - 1 1/4 cups (~300ml) Water (Liquid base)
    - 2/3 cup (~160ml) Whole Milk (Creaminess, body - User note: Trim milk okay)
    - 2/3 cup (~160ml) Heavy Cream (Richness, creaminess, body)
    - ~300g Gold Potatoes, diced into ~2cm cubes (Starch, body, texture - skin on okay)
    - 300g Boneless Skinless Chicken Breast, pre-diced (Protein, main component)
    - 1 2/3 cups (~185g) Sweet Corn (Frozen okay) (Sweetness, texture)
    - Desired Toppings (To taste) (Garnish, extra flavor/texture - see notes)

??? note "Equipment"
    - Large saucepan or Dutch oven with lid
    - Knife & Chopping board
    - Measuring cups & spoons
    - Wooden spoon or spatula
    - Stick blender (recommended) or standard blender/potato masher
    - Slotted spoon
    - Ladle

??? note "Preparation"
    1. Prep Bacon & Chicken: Cut bacon into small pieces. Ensure chicken is diced. Season diced chicken lightly with salt and pepper.
    2. Prep Vegetables: Dice onion and carrots. Dice jalapeño. Dice the roasted red pepper. Mince garlic.
    3. Prep Potatoes: Wash and dice potatoes into roughly 2cm cubes (leave skin on if using Gold potatoes).
    4. Measure: Have flour, spices, liquids (broth, water, milk, cream), and corn measured out.

??? note "Cooking Instructions"
    1. Cook Bacon: Place diced bacon in the large saucepan over medium heat. Cook until crisp (5-10 mins). Remove bacon with a slotted spoon to a paper towel-lined plate, leaving about 1-2 Tbsp of bacon grease in the pan. Set bacon aside.
    2. Cook Chicken: Add the seasoned diced chicken to the hot bacon grease in the pan. Sauté over medium-high heat until just cooked through (3-5 mins). Remove chicken with a slotted spoon and set aside with the crispy bacon. Add the 30g butter to the pan if needed.
    3. Sauté Aromatics (Base): Add the diced onion, carrots, and jalapeño to the fat in the pan. Cook over medium heat, stirring occasionally, until vegetables begin to soften (6-8 mins).
    4. Add Garlic & Roasted Pepper: Stir in the minced garlic and diced roasted red pepper. Cook until garlic is fragrant (about 30-60 seconds).
    5. Make Roux: Sprinkle the flour over the vegetables. Stir constantly until the flour is fully absorbed and lightly toasted (about 30 seconds to 1 minute).
    6. Add Seasonings & Liquids: Stir in the 3-4g kosher salt, 1/2 tsp black pepper, smoked paprika, and dry mustard powder. If using, add the Bay Leaf. Slowly whisk in the concentrated chicken broth and the water, ensuring no lumps form. Then, stir in the milk and cream.
    7. Add Potatoes & Corn: Add the diced potatoes and the sweet corn to the pot. Stir to combine.
    8. Simmer & Meld Flavors: Bring the mixture just to a boil, then immediately reduce heat to low, cover the saucepan with a lid, and maintain a gentle simmer. Cook for at least 25-30 minutes, stirring occasionally, to allow the potatoes to become fully tender and the flavours to meld. (Total simmer time can be longer if desired, just ensure potatoes don't turn to complete mush).
    9. Remove Bay Leaf & Blend: Remove and discard the Bay Leaf. Remove the pot from direct heat. Use a stick blender to carefully blend approximately half of the soup directly in the pot (blend the mixture of potatoes, vegetables, corn, and liquid). Blend until it reaches your desired creaminess, ensuring plenty of texture remains.
    10. Combine & Finish: Return the pot to low heat. Stir the pre-cooked diced chicken and the reserved crispy bacon back into the soup.
    11. Heat Through & Season: Heat gently for 2-3 minutes, just until the chicken and bacon are warmed through. Do not boil. Taste the chowder and adjust seasoning with more salt and pepper if needed.
    12. Rest (Optional): For potentially even better flavour melding, let the finished chowder sit off the heat, covered, for 5-10 minutes before serving.
    13. Serve: Ladle the hot chowder into bowls. Garnish with desired toppings.

    ??? info "Notes on Techniques, Ingredients, and Plating"
        - Extended Simmer: The longer, gentle simmering time (Step 8) allows for better flavour development and ensures potatoes are very tender. Keep the heat low to prevent scorching, especially with the lid on.
        - Bay Leaf: Essential to remove before blending/serving.
        - Other notes: Previous notes regarding chicken cooking, roasted pepper, carrots, blending, salt, liquid consistency, toppings, and plating still apply.
