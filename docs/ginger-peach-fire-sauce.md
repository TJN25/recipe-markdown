# Ginger-Peach Fire Sauce (Zambrero's Style)

Peach & Bird's Eye Hot Sauce (Zambrero-Inspired)
A spicy-sweet hot sauce featuring peaches, intense Bird's Eye chili heat, and ginger zing. Designed for heat lovers.
Yields: Approx. 350-450ml (1.5 - 2 cups)

??? note "Ingredients"
    - Peaches: 1 can (approx. 400-425g) peaches in juice or light syrup, drained (reserve juice)
    - Chillies: 1-3 Bird's Eye Chillies, stems removed (use 1 for hot, 2-3 for very hot - see notes)
    - Ginger: 1-2 tsp ginger paste (adjust to taste)
    - Garlic: 1-2 cloves garlic, smashed
    - Vinegar: 1/2 cup (120ml) apple cider vinegar
    - Sweetener: 1-3 tbsp brown sugar or honey (start with 1 tbsp, adjust based on peach sweetness)
    - Liquid: 1/4 cup (60ml) reserved peach juice OR water
    - Salt: 1/2 tsp salt, plus more to taste
    - Oil (Optional): 1 tsp neutral oil (e.g., canola, vegetable) for sautéing

??? note "Method"
    1. Preparation:
    - Drain canned peaches, reserving about 1/4 cup of the juice if desired (otherwise use water). Roughly chop the peaches.
    - Remove stems from Bird's Eye chillies. Roughly chop them. For slightly less intense heat, you can remove seeds and membranes before chopping.
    - Smash the garlic cloves.
    2. Sauté Aromatics (Optional but Recommended):
    - If using oil, heat it in a medium saucepan over medium heat.
    - Add the chopped Bird's Eye chillies and smashed garlic. Sauté for 1-2 minutes until fragrant.
    3. Combine & Simmer:
    - Add the chopped peaches, ginger paste, sautéed chillies and garlic (if using, otherwise add raw), apple cider vinegar, sweetener (start with 1 tbsp), reserved peach juice (or water), and 1/2 tsp salt to the saucepan.
    - Stir well to combine.
    - Bring the mixture to a gentle boil, then reduce the heat to low, cover, and simmer for 15-20 minutes, or until the peaches are very soft. Stir occasionally.
    4. Blend:
    - Carefully transfer the hot mixture to a blender. Be cautious when blending hot liquids: start on low speed and vent the lid slightly (cover vent with a tea towel) to prevent pressure build-up.
    - Blend until the sauce reaches your desired consistency (smooth or slightly chunky).
    5. Taste & Adjust:
    - Once blended, carefully taste the sauce.
    - Add more sweetener if needed (canned peaches vary).
    - Add more salt if required.
    - If you want more ginger flavour, blend in another 1/2 tsp of ginger paste.
    - If it's not hot enough (be brave!), you could add another chopped Bird's Eye chilli (or part of one) and blend again. Do this cautiously!
    - Adjust vinegar for more tang if desired.
    6. Finish:
    - Pour the sauce into clean jars or bottles.
    - Let it cool completely before sealing and refrigerating.

??? note "Storage"
    - Store in an airtight container or bottle in the refrigerator. Due to the vinegar, it should keep well for at least 2 weeks.
    - Can be frozen in portions for longer storage (up to 3 months). Thaw in the refrigerator.
    - Shake well before each use as some separation may occur.

    ??? info "Usage Notes & Tips"
        - Heat Warning: Bird's Eye chillies are significantly hotter than Serranos or Jalapeños. Start with 1 chilli if you are unsure of your heat tolerance. Removing seeds/membranes helps manage heat.
        - Ginger Paste: Potency varies. Adjust the amount based on how prominent you want the ginger flavour.
        - Sweetness: Canned peaches packed in syrup will require less added sweetener than those in juice. Always taste before adding the full amount.
        - Consistency: If the sauce is too thick after blending, thin it with a little more water, reserved peach juice, or vinegar. If too thin before blending, simmer uncovered for a few more minutes to reduce.
        - Flavour: Flavours will meld and develop further after a day or two in the fridge.

        Attempt 1.
        Ginger-Peach Fire Sauce (Zambrero's Style)
        A spicy-sweet-zingy sauce with ginger warmth

??? note "Ingredients"
    - 2 tbsp avocado oil
    - 2 tbsp flour
    - 3 tbsp fresh ginger, finely grated (a little too much!)
    - 4 garlic cloves, minced
    - 2-3 fresh hot chilies
    - 2 canned peach halves (about 1/3 cup), drained and diced
    - 3/4 cup chicken stock
    - 1.5 tbsp white vinegar
    - 1 tbsp soy sauce
    - 1/2 tsp cardamom
    - 1/2 tsp brown sugar (taste first - might not need with canned peaches)
    - 1/2 tsp salt

??? note "Preparation"
    1. Drain peaches, pat dry, dice
    2. Finely grate ginger
    3. Mince garlic
    4. Finely dice chilies
    5. Have stock ready

??? note "Method"
    1. Heat oil in saucepan over medium heat
    2. Add flour, whisk until golden (2-3 mins)
    3. Add ginger and garlic, cook 1-2 mins
    4. Add chilies, cook another minute
    5. Add diced peaches, cook just 1 min
    6. Gradually whisk in stock
    7. Add vinegar, soy sauce, cardamom, salt
    8. Simmer 15-20 mins, stirring occasionally
    9. Taste, add brown sugar if needed
    10. Blend until completely smooth
    11. Strain through fine mesh
    12. Cool completely

??? note "Storage"
    - Refrigerate up to 1 week
    - Freeze excess in portions
    - Bring to room temp before using
    - Shake well before each use

??? note "Notes"
    - Start with less chili, can add more
    - If too thick, add stock
    - If too thin, simmer longer
    - Makes about 1.5-2 cups
