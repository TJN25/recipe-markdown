# Chicken Mushroom Pasta

A quick and flavourful weeknight pasta dish featuring tender chicken and mushrooms in a creamy sauce using white wine vinegar and cream cheese.
**Prep:** 10 minutes
**Cook:** 20 minutes
**Total:** 30 minutes
**Serves:** 2 people

??? note "Ingredients"
    - Dried Pasta: 140-150 g (e.g., fettuccine, penne, linguine)
    - Boneless, Skinless Chicken Breast: ~150g, diced Onion: 1 small
    - Fresh Mushrooms: ~115 g, finely diced (e.g., cremini, button)
    - Garlic: 2 cloves minced
    - Unsalted Butter: 20g
    - Oil
    - Salt: To taste
    - Black Pepper: To taste (freshly ground recommended)
    - White Wine Vinegar: 15 ml (1 Tbsp) (Substitution for white wine)
    - Balsamic vinegar 5 ml
    - Chicken Stock: 100 ml (Increased slightly to compensate for less vinegar)
    - Full-Fat Cream Cheese: ~120g, softened (Substitution for heavy cream)
    - Reserved Pasta Water: ~120 ml (1/2 cup), as needed to adjust sauce consistency

??? note "Method"
    1. Cook Pasta: Cook the pasta according to package directions until al dente. Before draining, reserve about 120 ml (1/2 cup) of the starchy cooking water. Drain the pasta and set aside.
    2. Cook Chicken: season with salt and pepper, let rest then broil for 5 minutes
    3. Sauté Aromatics & Mushrooms: In the same skillet (add a splash more oil if dry), cook the chopped onion over medium heat until softened and lightly browned, about 2-3 minutes. Add the sliced mushrooms and cook until tender and they've released their liquid, about 3-4 minutes.
    4. Deglaze: Add the white wine vinegar to the pan. Let it bubble and scrape up any browned bits from the bottom of the skillet. Simmer for about 30 seconds until the strong vinegar smell mellows slightly.
    5. Build Sauce Base: Pour in the chicken stock. Bring to a simmer.
    6. Add Cream Cheese and butter: Turn the heat down to low or remove the pan from the heat temporarily. Add the softened cream cheese. Whisk vigorously until the cream cheese melts smoothly into the stock mixture, creating a creamy sauce. Do not boil rapidly after adding cream cheese.
    7. Combine & Finish: Return the cooked chicken (and any accumulated juices) to the skillet with the sauce. Stir to coat. Let it heat through gently for about 30-60 seconds.
    8. Adjust Consistency: Stir in the cooked pasta. If the sauce is too thick, gradually add some of the reserved pasta water, a tablespoon at a time, until it reaches your desired consistency. The starch in the pasta water helps emulsify the sauce.
    9. Season & Serve: Taste the sauce and adjust seasoning with salt and pepper if needed. Serve immediately, topped with grated parmesan
