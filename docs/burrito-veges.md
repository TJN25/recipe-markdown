
# Enchilada-Style Vegetable Burrito Component

**Makes:** about 4 portions

??? note "Ingredients"

    * 1 tbsp olive oil
    * 1 onion, sliced
    * 1 red capsicum, sliced
    * 1 carrot, grated or cut into small matchsticks
    * 100 g frozen corn
    * 1 garlic clove, finely minced
    * 2 tbsp sliced pickled jalapeños, drained
    * 1–2 tbsp water

    ??? info "Seasoning"

        * 1/2 tsp onion powder
        * 1/2 tsp garlic powder
        * 1/2 tsp salt
        * 1 tsp cumin
        * 1 tsp paprika
        * 1 tsp dried oregano
        * 1/8 tsp black pepper
        * cayenne, to taste

??? note "Method"

    1. Heat the oil in a large pan over **medium heat**.

    2. Add the onion, capsicum, and carrot. Cook for 6–8 minutes until softened.

    3. Add the garlic and cook for about 30 seconds.

    4. Add the onion powder, garlic powder, salt, cumin, paprika, oregano, and cayenne. Stir for about 15 seconds.

    5. Add the water, corn, and jalapeños. Stir well and cook for 2–3 minutes until the corn is hot and everything is evenly coated.

    6. Stir through the black pepper.

    7. Let cool before portioning and freezing.

    8. Reheat in an oven dish covered with foil at **200°C** until hot, alongside the chicken.

??? note "Notes"

    This is intended as a separate burrito component. 

    Keeping it fairly dry should suit freezing, thawing, and oven reheating better for this use.
