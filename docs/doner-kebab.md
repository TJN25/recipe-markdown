# Donee Kebab

**Prep:** 10 minutes
**Cook:** 8 minutes per batch (4 batches total)
**Total:** 40-45 minutes
**Serves:** 2

---

??? note "Ingredients"

    ??? info "For the Kebab:"

        - **450g ground beef** (protein, fat - provides structure and flavor; use 80/20 lean-to-fat ratio for best results)
        - **½ medium onion, finely grated** (moisture, sweetness - helps keep meat tender)
        - **2 cloves garlic, minced** (aromatic, flavor)
        - **30g Greek yogurt** (tenderizer, moisture - breaks down proteins for tender meat)
        - **1 tsp salt** (salty - enhances all flavors)
        - **½ tsp black pepper** (spice, heat)
        - **1 tsp cumin** (earthy, warm flavor)
        - **1 tsp paprika** (color, mild sweetness)
        - **½ tsp coriander** (citrusy, aromatic)
        - **¼ tsp cayenne pepper** (heat - optional, adjust to taste)
        - **½ tsp dried oregano** (herbal, Mediterranean flavor)
        - sprinkle of sumac

        ---

??? note "Method"

    ??? info "Preparation "

        1. Finely grate onion using box grater over a bowl. Squeeze out excess liquid through fine mesh strainer or in clean tea towel - this is crucial to prevent mixture from being too wet. Mince garlic using microplane or small knife on plastic chopping board. Measure all spices into a ramekin.
        2. While meat rests, shred lettuce, slice tomatoes and onion on large wooden chopping board. Transfer to serving plates or bowls.
        3. In a medium metal bowl, add ground beef, grated onion (liquid squeezed out), minced garlic, Greek yogurt, and all spices.
        4. Using clean hands, mix vigorously for 3-4 minutes until mixture becomes sticky and paste-like. This develops the proteins and creates the characteristic doner texture. The mixture should be very well combined and slightly tacky.
         
         *At this point it can be portioned up and frozen.*

    ??? info "Cooking Stage (125g batches):"

        1. Heat oven to 200C.
        2. Take portion and roll flat on baking paper
        3. Roll up in the baking paper like a log
        4. Place in oven for 15 minutes
        5. Rmove and tear up. Can add back in with oven set to grill for 2-3 minutes (but I don't usually bother).

        Alterante:
        1. Preheat cast iron pan on medium high heat (as hot as can be handled before smoke in the room).
        3. Flatten portion into a thin, even patty approximately 15-18cm diameter and 1-1.5cm thick. Press firmly to compact.
        4. Place patty in dry cast iron pan (no oil needed due to fat content). Cook for 3-4 minutes without moving. Patty should develop a golden-brown crust.
        5. Using plastic spatula, carefully flip patty. Cook second side for 3-4 minutes until cooked through and internal temperature reaches 70-75°C if checked with food thermometer.
