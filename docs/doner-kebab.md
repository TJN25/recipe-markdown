
# Donee Kebab

**Prep:** 10 minutes
**Cook:** 8 minutes per batch (4 batches total)
**Total:** 40-45 minutes
**Serves:** 2

---

??? note "Ingredients"

    ??? info "For the Kebab:"

        - **450g ground beef** (protein, fat - provides structure and flavor; use 80/20 lean-to-fat ratio for best results)
        - **½ medium onion, finely grated** (moisture, sweetness - helps keep meat tender)
        - **2 cloves garlic, minced** (aromatic, flavor)
        - **30g Greek yogurt** (tenderizer, moisture - breaks down proteins for tender meat)
        - **1 tsp salt** (salty - enhances all flavors)
        - **½ tsp black pepper** (spice, heat)
        - **1 tsp cumin** (earthy, warm flavor)
        - **1 tsp paprika** (color, mild sweetness)
        - **½ tsp coriander** (citrusy, aromatic)
        - **¼ tsp cayenne pepper** (heat - optional, adjust to taste)
        - **½ tsp dried oregano** (herbal, Mediterranean flavor)
        - sprinkle of sumac

        ---

??? note "Method"

    ??? info "Prep Stage:"

        1. **Prepare ingredients:** Finely grate onion using box grater over a bowl. Squeeze out excess liquid through fine mesh strainer or in clean tea towel - this is crucial to prevent mixture from being too wet. Mince garlic using microplane or small knife on plastic chopping board. Measure all spices into a ramekin.
        2. **Prepare vegetables:** While meat rests, shred lettuce, slice tomatoes and onion on large wooden chopping board. Transfer to serving plates or bowls.

    ??? info "Mixing Stage:"

        1. **Combine ingredients:** In a medium metal bowl, add ground beef, grated onion (liquid squeezed out), minced garlic, Greek yogurt, and all spices.
        2. **Mix thoroughly:** Using clean hands, mix vigorously for 3-4 minutes until mixture becomes sticky and paste-like. This develops the proteins and creates the characteristic doner texture. The mixture should be very well combined and slightly tacky.
        3. **Rest:** Cover bowl and refrigerate for 10-15 minutes while you prepare serving ingredients. This helps mixture firm up for easier handling.

    ??? info "Cooking Stage (125g batches):"

        1. **Preheat pan:** Heat 24cm cast iron pan over medium heat for 2-3 minutes until evenly hot.
        2. **Divide mixture:** Divide mixture into 4 portions of approximately 125g each (you can use scales for accuracy or eyeball it).
        3. **Form first patty:** Take first portion and flatten into a thin, even patty approximately 15-18cm diameter and 1-1.5cm thick. Press firmly to compact.
        4. **Cook first side:** Place patty in dry cast iron pan (no oil needed due to fat content). Cook for 3-4 minutes without moving. Patty should develop a golden-brown crust.
        5. **Flip and cook:** Using plastic spatula, carefully flip patty. Cook second side for 3-4 minutes until cooked through and internal temperature reaches 70-75°C if checked with food thermometer.
        6. **Rest and repeat:** Transfer to chopping board and tent loosely with foil. Repeat with remaining 3 portions, keeping cooked patties warm under foil.

    ??? info "Slicing and Assembly:"

        1. **Slice meat:** Using large chef's knife, slice all patties into thin strips approximately 5mm wide, mimicking traditional doner kebab slices.
        2. **Optional crisping:** If desired, return sliced meat to hot cast iron pan for 30-60 seconds to crisp edges.
        3. **Warm bread:** Heat pita or flatbreads in toaster oven at 180°C convection for 2-3 minutes, or briefly in the cast iron pan after removing meat.
        4. **Build kebabs:** Layer warm bread with sliced meat, shredded lettuce, tomatoes, onions, and generous drizzle of tzatziki or garlic sauce. Garnish with fresh herbs.

        ---

??? note "Suggestions for Enhancement"

    - **Extra crispy edges:** After slicing, increase heat to medium-high and sear meat strips for 30-60 seconds, tossing frequently.
    - **Spice blend upgrade:** Toast whole cumin and coriander seeds in dry cast iron pan until fragrant, then grind in pestle and mortar for more intense flavor. Do this before cooking meat.
    - **Onion technique:** After grating, squeeze onion in clean tea towel to remove maximum moisture - this prevents the meat from being too wet and helps achieve better crust.
    - **Marinating time:** Mix ingredients and refrigerate for 1-2 hours (or up to overnight) before cooking to allow flavors to meld.
    - **Pan temperature:** If patty is sticking or burning, reduce heat slightly. If not browning well, increase heat.
    - **Thickness matters:** Keep patties uniformly thin for even cooking and easier slicing into strips.

    ---

??? note "Storage and Leftovers"

    - **Refrigerate raw:** Store uncooked mixture in airtight container for up to 24 hours before cooking.
    - **Refrigerate cooked:** Store cooked, sliced meat in airtight container for up to 3 days.
    - **Freeze raw:** Freeze uncooked mixture divided into 125g portions wrapped tightly in plastic wrap for up to 3 months. Thaw in refrigerator overnight before cooking.
    - **Reheat:** Warm sliced meat in cast iron pan over medium heat for 1-2 minutes, or in toaster oven at 180°C for 3-4 minutes.
    - **Repurpose:** Use leftover meat in wraps, over rice bowls, in salads, with eggs for breakfast, or in toasted sandwiches.

    ---

??? note "Ingredient Substitutions"

    - **Ground lamb:** Replace beef with lamb for more traditional flavor, or use 50/50 beef and lamb blend (225g each).
    - **Ground chicken or turkey:** Use for leaner option, but add 1 tbsp olive oil to mixture to prevent dryness and brush pan lightly with oil before cooking.
    - **Yogurt:** Substitute with sour cream or omit and add 1 tbsp olive oil instead.
    - **Fresh herbs:** Add 2 tbsp finely chopped fresh parsley or mint to meat mixture for brightness.
    - **Onion:** Use shallot for milder, sweeter flavor.

    ---

??? note ""

    **Ingredient Notes:**
    - **Fat content:** 80/20 ground beef is ideal. Leaner meat will be dry and may stick to pan; fattier will shrink too much and be greasy.
    - **Onion moisture:** Removing excess liquid is crucial - too much moisture will make the meat mixture loose, prevent proper browning, and cause steaming instead of searing.
    - **Yogurt acidity:** The lactic acid in yogurt tenderizes the meat while adding subtle tang. Don't skip it.
    - **Spice ratios:** This blend is moderately spiced. Adjust cayenne and black pepper to your heat preference.
    - **Room temperature:** If mixture has been refrigerated, let sit at room temperature for 5-10 minutes before cooking for more even cooking.

    **Additional Ingredients to Consider:**
    - **Sumac:** Sprinkle over finished kebab for tangy, lemony flavor
    - **Aleppo pepper:** Use instead of cayenne for fruity heat
    - **Fresh mint:** Add 1 tbsp chopped to meat mixture or as garnish
    - **Pomegranate molasses:** Drizzle over assembled kebab for sweet-tart contrast
    - **Pickled vegetables:** Serve with pickled turnips, cucumbers, or peppers (available at Woolworths NZ in international aisle)
    - **Chili sauce:** Add sriracha or harissa for extra heat
    - **Lemon wedges:** Serve alongside for squeezing over finished kebab

    **Plating and Presentation:**
    - **Wrap style:** Wrap kebab in parchment paper cone for authentic street food presentation - fold bottom to catch drips
    - **Plate style:** Arrange sliced meat in overlapping rows on white plate, surround with fresh vegetables, and place sauce in small ramekin on side
    - **Bowl style:** Create a kebab bowl with base of shredded lettuce, top with warm meat, vegetables, and drizzle of sauce
    - **Color contrast:** Use red tomatoes, green lettuce, white sauce, and purple onion for vibrant visual appeal
    - **Garnish:** Finish with sprinkle of sumac, fresh herbs, and drizzle of olive oil
    - **Serve warm:** Always serve meat warm or hot for best flavor and texture
    - **Sauce presentation:** Drizzle sauce in zigzag pattern over top, or serve in squeeze bottle for control
