
# Meat Pie Recipe

??? note "Ingredients"
    ??? info "Filling Ingredients"
        - Beef mince (500g) - protein base, umami
        - Brown onion (1 large) - sweetness, flavor foundation
        - Garlic (1 tsp crushed, or 2 fresh cloves) - aromatic depth
        - Beef stock cube (1) + water (250ml) - umami, moisture
        - Tomato paste (1 Tbsp) - acidity, color, umami
        - Worcestershire sauce (1 Tbsp) - tangy umami, complexity
        - Vegemite (1 tsp) - intense umami, saltiness
        - Italian herbs (1 tsp dried) - aromatic complexity
        - Cornflour (2 tsp) + water (60ml) - thickening agent

    ??? info "Pastry & Topping"
        - Puff pastry (3 sheets, thawed) - crispy texture, buttery flavor
        - Cheese slices (4-6 slices tasty cheese) - creamy richness, browning


??? note "Method"
    ??? info "Prep Stage (15 minutes):"
        1. Thaw pastry at room temperature while prepping
        2. Dice onion finely (5mm pieces) using large chopping board
        3. Mince garlic fresh or use crushed
        4. Dissolve stock cube in 250ml hot water, set aside
        5. Mix cornflour slurry in small bowl, set aside
        6. Preheat oven to 185°C conventional (or 165°C fan-forced)
    ??? info "Cooking Stage (25 minutes):"
        1. Heat large nonstick pan over medium-high heat
        2. Brown mince in batches (don't overcrowd) - 4-5 minutes per batch
        3. Remove mince, reduce heat to medium
        4. Cook onion in same pan until softened (3-4 minutes)
        5. Add garlic, cook 30 seconds until fragrant
        6. Return mince to pan with tomato paste, cook 1 minute
        7. Add stock, Worcestershire, Vegemite, herbs - stir well
        8. Simmer 10-12 minutes until reduced by half
        9. Stir cornflour slurry, add to pan, cook 2 minutes until thickened
        10. Season with salt and pepper, cool 5 minutes
    ??? info "Assembly & Baking (45-50 minutes):"
        1. Line casserole dish with baking paper
        2. Lay 1.5 pastry sheets as base, pressing into corners
        3. Trim excess pastry, leaving 2cm overhang
        4. Add cooled filling, level surface
        5. Arrange cheese slices evenly over filling
        6. Cover with remaining pastry, seal edges with fork
        7. Cut 3-4 steam vents in top
        8. Brush with beaten egg (optional, for golden color)
        9. Bake 40-50 minutes until golden brown and crispy

    ??? info "Enhancement Suggestions"

    ??? info "Flavor Improvements"
        - Add diced carrot (1 medium) with onion for sweetness and nutrition
        - Use fresh thyme instead of dried Italian herbs
        - Deglaze pan with red wine (2 Tbsp) before adding stock
        - Add bay leaf during simmering, remove before serving

    ??? info "Texture Enhancements"
        - Partially freeze mince for 30 minutes before cooking for better browning
        - Use 50/50 beef and pork mince for richer flavor
        - Add diced bacon (2 rashers) for extra richness

    ??? info "Portion Control for 1-2 Servings"
        - Make full recipe, portion into 3 smaller dishes
        - Freeze unbaked portions for future meals
        - Use individual ramekins for single servings

    ??? info "Leftover Management"
        - Cooked filling keeps 3 days refrigerated, 3 months frozen
        - Leftover pastry can be used for sausage rolls or cheese twists
        - Reheat slices in toaster oven at 180°C for 10-15 minutes

    ??? info "Storage Instructions"
        - Cool completely before refrigerating
        - Wrap individual portions in foil for easy reheating
        - Freeze whole pie up to 3 months (thaw before reheating)

    ??? info "Notes on Techniques, Ingredients, and Plating"

    ??? info "Advanced Techniques"
        - Pre-cook pastry base for 10 minutes to prevent soggy bottom
        - Use egg wash between pastry layers for better sealing
        - Rest assembled pie 15 minutes before baking for better structure

    ??? info "Ingredient Substitutions"
        - Replace Vegemite with Marmite or additional Worcestershire
        - Use lamb mince for more distinctive flavor
        - Add mushrooms (150g diced) for earthiness and extra vegetables

    ??? info "Plating and Presentation"
        - Serve with mushy peas and mashed potato for traditional presentation
        - Garnish with fresh herbs (parsley or thyme)
        - Cut clean slices using sharp knife, wiping between cuts
        - Serve on warmed plates with contrasting vegetables for color

    ??? info "Professional Tips"
        - Use kitchen scales for accurate cornflour measurement
        - Test doneness by inserting skewer - should come out clean
        - Let rest 5 minutes before cutting for cleaner slices
