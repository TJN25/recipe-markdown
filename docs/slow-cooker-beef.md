
# Guajillo Braised Chuck Roast

**Serves:** 4
**Prep:** 1 hour | **Cook:** 4 hours | **Total:** 5 hours

??? note "Ingredients"

    - 1 kg chuck roast, trimmed and cut into 8-10 cm chunks
    - 4 dried guajillo peppers
    - 1½ tsp ground cumin
    - ½ tsp black pepper (plus more for seasoning)
    - 1½ tsp salt (plus more for seasoning)
    - ½ tsp cayenne pepper (optional)
    - 1½ tsp smoked paprika
    - ¼ white onion, finely chopped
    - 5 cloves garlic, grated
    - 1 tbsp tomato paste
    - 2 bay leaves
    - 240 ml beef stock (low-sodium)
    - Olive oil for searing

??? note "Instructions"

    1. Remove stems and seeds from peppers. Soak in hot water weighted down with a plate for 30 minutes until soft.
    2. With a pestle and mortar blend softened peppers with cumin, black pepper, salt, cayenne, smoked paprika, and ¼ cup soaking water until smooth paste forms.
    3. Season beef chunks generously with salt and pepper.
    4. Heat Dutch oven over high heat with oil. Brown beef in batches on all sides, about 3-4 minutes per side. Remove and reserve.
    5. Reduce heat to medium. Cook onion until translucent, 3-4 minutes. Add garlic and tomato paste, cook 1 minute. Add pepper paste, cook 2-3 minutes until fragrant, scraping up fond.
    6. Return beef and any juices to pot. Add stock to come halfway up the beef. Add bay leaves. Cover and braise at 175°C for 3-4 hours until fork-tender. Check at 2.5 hours to ensure liquid hasn't reduced too much.
    7. Remove bay leaves before serving. Shred or serve in chunks.
