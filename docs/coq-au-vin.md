# Coq au Vin

A luxurious French chicken stew braised in red wine with bacon, mushrooms, and pearl onions.

**Prep:** 15 minutes
**Cook:** 1 hour 30 minutes
**Marination:** 12-24 hours
**Total:** 1 hour 45 minutes (plus marination)
**Serves:** 2

??? note "Ingredients"
    ??? info "Marinade"
        - 2 chicken thighs, bone-in, skin-on (220g each)
        - 2 chicken drumsticks
        - 8 pearl onions (or 1 regular onion, wedged)
        - 1 bay leaf
        - 2 thyme sprigs
        - 375ml (1½ cups) Pinot Noir or dry red wine
    ??? info "For Cooking"
        - 2 tbsp vegetable oil
        - ¼ tsp black pepper
        - ⅜ tsp kosher salt
        - 200g white mushrooms, halved
        - 75g bacon, cut into lardons
        - 30g unsalted butter
        - 2 garlic cloves, minced
        - 1 tbsp tomato paste
        - 3½ tbsp all-purpose flour
        - 375ml (1½ cups) beef stock
        - Fresh parsley for garnish
??? note "Method"

    ??? info "Day Before"
        1. Combine chicken, wine, onions, bay leaf, and thyme in glass container
        2. Refrigerate 12-24 hours

??? note "Preparation"
    1. Remove chicken from marinade, pat very dry
    2. Strain and reserve wine and onions
    3. Reduce wine by half in saucepan, set aside
    4. Preheat oven to 180°C (350°F)

??? note "Cooking"
    1. Season chicken with salt and pepper
    2. Heat oil in heavy pot over medium-high heat
    3. Brown chicken skin-side down (2-3 minutes), flip (1 minute)
    4. Remove chicken, cook bacon until golden (3 minutes)
    5. Sauté mushrooms until golden (5 minutes)
    6. Cook onions until golden patches appear (5 minutes)
    7. Add butter, garlic, cook 1 minute
    8. Add tomato paste, cook 2 minutes
    9. Add flour, cook 2 minutes
    10. Slowly stir in stock, then reduced wine
    11. Return chicken, bacon, mushrooms to pot
    12. Cover, transfer to oven for 45 minutes
    ??? info "Finishing"
        1. Check sauce thickness, reduce if needed
        2. Adjust seasoning
        3. Rest overnight (recommended)
        4. Reheat gently before serving
        5. Garnish with parsley

??? note "Notes"
    - Best made day ahead
    - Use Pinot Noir or light-bodied red wine
    - Beef stock preferred for richer color
    - Serve with mashed potatoes or tagliatelle
    ??? info "Pro Tips"
        - Pat chicken very dry for better browning
        - Cook ingredients separately for best flavor
        - Keep sauce slightly thinner than desired as it will thicken while cooling
        - Use homemade beef stock if possible
