
# GYOZA (8 pieces)

??? note "Ingredients"

    ??? info "Filling"
        - 1/3 cup purple cabbage, very finely chopped *(texture, moisture)*
        - 1/4 tsp salt *(draws out water from cabbage)*
        - 100 g chicken thigh, skin removed, minced or very finely chopped *(fat, juicy)*
        - 3 tbsp spring onion (green parts), finely chopped *(aromatic, fresh)*
        - 1 garlic clove, crushed *(aromatic)*
        - 1/4 tsp ginger powder *(warm spice)*
        - 1/4 tsp sesame oil *(nutty, aromatic)*
        - 1 tsp cornflour *(helps bind and retain moisture)*
        - 1/2 tsp soy sauce *(salty, umami)*

    ??? info "Wrappers & Cooking"
        - 8 round wonton wrappers (gyoza/gow gee wrappers)
        - Cornflour for dusting tray *(prevents sticking)*
        - 1 tbsp neutral oil (vegetable or rice bran) *(for frying)*

    ??? info "Dipping Sauce (per person)"
        - 1 tbsp soy sauce
        - 1 tbsp rice vinegar *(sharpness, cuts richness)*
        - Few drops chilli oil or sriracha *(heat, optional)*

??? note "Preparation"

    ??? info "Make the filling (can be done 1 day ahead)"
        1. Mix chopped purple cabbage with 1/4 tsp salt in a small bowl. Toss to coat. Set aside for 20 minutes (cabbage will wilt and release water).
        2. After 20 minutes, grab handfuls of cabbage and squeeze firmly over the sink to remove as much liquid as possible. Purple cabbage holds more water than green; be thorough or filling will be wet. Set squeezed cabbage aside.
        3. In a medium metal bowl, combine chicken thigh, spring onion, garlic, ginger, sesame oil, cornflour, and soy sauce.
        4. Add squeezed cabbage. Mix with your hands (or a spoon if you prefer) until evenly combined. The filling should be slightly sticky and hold together when pressed. Cover and refrigerate until ready to wrap (up to 24 hours).

    ??? info "Wrap the gyoza"
        1. Dust a large chopping board or baking tray lightly with cornflour.
        2. Set out a small bowl of water.
        3. Place one wrapper on your palm (left hand if right-handed). Dip your finger in water and run it around the top half of the wrapper edge.
        4. Place 1 heaped tsp filling in the centre (don't overfill or it will burst).
        5. Fold the wrapper in half to form a half-moon. Pinch the centre to seal.
        6. Using your dominant hand, create 3–4 pleats on the front side only, pressing each pleat firmly to seal it to the flat back. The gyoza should curve slightly and sit upright on a flat base.
        7. Place finished gyoza on the dusted tray. Cover with a damp tea towel to prevent drying out. Repeat with remaining wrappers.

        *Watch the RecipeTin Eats video linked in the original for a visual guide to pleating—it's much easier to learn by watching than reading.*

??? note "Cooking"

    ??? info "Pan-fry and steam method (most authentic)"
        1. Heat 1 tbsp oil in the small or large nonstick pan over medium-high heat. Swirl to coat the base.
        2. Arrange gyoza in the pan in a single layer, flat side down, slightly overlapping if needed (they'll crisp and separate). Let them fry undisturbed for 2–3 minutes until the bottoms are golden brown.
        3. Carefully add 1/4 cup water to the pan (it will splatter—pour at the edge, not directly onto gyoza). Immediately cover with a lid.
        4. Reduce heat to medium. Steam for 4–5 minutes until the water has completely evaporated and the wrappers look translucent and slightly puffed.
        5. Remove lid. Let gyoza cook uncovered for another 1–2 minutes to re-crisp the bottoms if they've softened.
        6. Use an egg flip to transfer gyoza to a plate, golden side up.
