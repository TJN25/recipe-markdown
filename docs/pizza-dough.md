# Pizza Dough

??? note "Double Batch Pizza Dough - 24 Hour Cold Ferment"

    **Makes:** 2 pizzas, using weights

    ??? info "Ingredients"
        *75% hydration*
        - 280g bread flour (structure, gluten development)
        - 210g warm water at 40-46°C (75% hydration)
        - 3/4 tsp active dry yeast (reduced for long cold ferment) 4-5 g
        - 3/4 tsp raw sugar (yeast food, browning) 5g
        - 5g salt (1 tsp) (flavor, fermentation control)
        - 8g olive oil (tenderness, flavor)

    ??? info "Method"

        #### Day 1 - Prep (24 hours before cooking)
        1. **Yeast Activation**
        - Mix warm water, yeast, and sugar
        - Let stand 10-15 minutes until foamy

        2. **Make Dough**
        - Mix flour and salt in bowl
        - Add yeast mixture and olive oil
        - Mix with fork until no dry flour remains
        - Proceed immediately to stretch and fold

        3. **Initial Development**
        - First stretch and fold
        - Rest 30 minutes
        - Second stretch and fold
        - Rest 30 minutes

        4. **Cold Ferment Setup**
        - Total dough weight ≈ 490g
        - Divide into two 245g portions
        - Shape into balls using edge-folding technique
        - Place each portion in a lightly oiled container
        - Refrigerate for 24 hours

        #### Day 2 - Cooking
        1. **Pizza**
        - Remove first dough portion 30-45 minutes before cooking
        - Let warm up at room temperature
        - Prepare toppings while waiting
        - Cold cast iron pan + cooking spray
        - Stretch dough (rest if resistant)
        - Top with sauce and ingredients
        - **Cooking Process Refined:**
        - Preheat oven to 250°C fan bake
        - Medium-high stovetop (slightly hotter than last time)
        - Cook 6-7 minutes on stovetop (watch for slight bubbling on top)
        - Transfer to oven for 10-12 minutes initially

        #### Check at 10 minutes - look for
        - Cheese fully melted and starting to brown
        - Crust edge golden brown
        - Bottom crispy (lift with spatula to check)
        - Add 2-minute intervals if needed
        - Rest 2-3 minutes before cutting

    ??? info "Notes"
        - Overproofed when done overnight.
        - Good when started at 3 and cooked at 7
        - Stretch and folds done every 30 minutes for 3 sets starting after a 10 minute rest of the shaggy dough

??? note "Pizza Dough - Same Day Preparation"

    **Makes:** 2 pizzas, 12-14" each

    ??? info "Ingredients"
        75% hydration
        - 280g bread flour (structure, gluten development)
        - 210g warm water at 40-46°C (75% hydration)
        - 4-5g active dry yeast (3/4 tsp)
        - 5g raw sugar (3/4 tsp) (yeast food, browning)
        - 5g salt (1 tsp) (flavor, fermentation control)
        - 8g olive oil (tenderness, flavor)

    ??? info "Method"
        Same Day Preparation (4-5 hours before cooking)
        - Yeast Activation (10-15 minutes)
        - Mix warm water (40-43°C), yeast, and sugar
        - Let stand 10-15 minutes until foamy
        - Make Dough (10 minutes)
        - Mix flour and salt in bowl
        - Add yeast mixture and olive oil
        - Mix with fork until no dry flour remains
        - Rest 10 minutes (shaggy dough)
        - Initial Development (2 hours)
        - First stretch and fold
        - Rest 30 minutes
        - Second stretch and fold
        - Rest 30 minutes
        - Third stretch and fold
        - Rest 30 minutes
        - Final stretch and fold (if needed)
        - Rest 30 minutes
        - Dividing and Shaping
        - Total dough weight ≈ 490g
        - Divide into two 245g portions
        - Shape into balls using edge-folding technique
        - Rest at room temperature until ready to use
        - Cooking Process
        - Prepare toppings while dough completes final rise
        - Cold cast iron pan + cooking spray
        - Stretch dough (rest if resistant)
        - Top with sauce and ingredients

        #### Cooking Process
        - Preheat oven to 250°C fan bake
        - Medium-high stovetop
        - Cook 5-7 minutes on stovetop (watch for slight bubbling on top)
        - Transfer to oven for 10-12 minutes initially
        - Check at 10 minutes for:
        - Cheese fully melted and starting to brown
        - Crust edge golden brown
        - Bottom crispy (lift with spatula to check)
        - Add 2-minute intervals if needed
        - Rest 2-3 minutes before cutting

    ??? info "Notes for Overnight Fermentation"
        If you want to adapt this recipe for overnight fermentation, make these adjustments:
        1. Yeast Reduction
        Reduce yeast to 1-2g (about 1/4 to 1/3 tsp) to prevent overproofing
        2. Initial Development
        Complete only 1-2 sets of stretch and folds before refrigeration
        Total room temperature time should be 1 hour maximum before refrigerating
        3. Timing
        Start dough around 3pm for cooking at 7pm the next day
        This timing worked well in your previous attempts
        4. Temperature
        Keep all temperatures the same as the successful same-day recipe
        The reduced yeast amount will control fermentation rate
        5. Handling Overproofed Dough
        If dough appears overproofed (very bubbly, loose structure):
        Handle extremely gently to preserve gas
        Use minimal stretching to avoid deflation
        Consider using as focaccia-style pizza if too delicate to stretch
        6. Cold Dough Handling
        Remove from refrigerator 45-60 minutes before cooking
        If dough is still cold and tight when stretching, allow more time to warm up
