# First experiences were baking and microwaving

School covered some simple mince meals in Cooking
High school, learned dough, calzones (nutella), samosas, focaccia, and had a go at mayo and burger patties.

Started out on my own with the goal to make recipes I enjoyed.
Spent ~$30 per meal to feed ~4 (2012).

    ??? info "Began with"
        - Mexican Calzones and making my own salsa (eventually just bought salsa)
        - Satay Pizza (made satay sauce, hated it, bought it from then on)
        - Mexican (beef mince pizza), was very spicy
        - Chicken curry (first thing done in the oven at all)
        - Potato curry (learned how long potatoes take to cook)
        - Naan (first experience of anything other than ‘pizza’ dough)
        - Had a go at pastry (did not work well)
        - Had a couple of attempts at croissants. Got the texture right once, and the taste right once, but not together
        - Made some apple pies and apple crumbles
        - Made some risottos, they took a very long time
        - Made a tomato/cream pasta and sauce
        - Made a chicken salad

    ??? info "Continued with"
        - Tandoori pastry version of calzones
        - Bacon and egg pie (did not work well)
        - Custard square (runny custard did not work)
        - Vegan mac and cheese
        Throughout this, had a go making subs, burgers, scrambled eggs.
        Learned knife skills, confidence in cooking pasta, potato, rice, chicken, beef
        General confidence in stovetop cooking.
        Stayed away from anything in the oven, deep frying, and desserts etc.
        Had a number of goes at breads and they were always very dense.

        Lots of attempts at ‘Butter chicken’ that did not work out well.

        Learned that the space to cook in, and having simple things on hand is important.
        - Sharp knives
        - Big chopping boards
        - Space to cook
        - Immersion blender
        - Many different sized bowls
        - Squeeze bottles
        - Paper towels
        - Clean as you go
        - Prepare in advance

        Came across cookwell and also started using enough salt, and including acid. Accepted that some meals wouldn’t quite work out.
        Changed the approach from ‘making a recipe’ to ‘aiming to make something’ which is subtle, but involved having a general goal and making it work with what I had. Sometimes this involved seeking out a recipe and following it to a tee (butter chicken finally made successfully like this).
        Taking notes about what went wrong and seeking out specifics on how to improve them.
        Taking a single idea e.g. making [pasta](recipes/fresh-egg-pasta.md), and [building a meal](recipes/fettuccine-with-mushroom-white-wine-sauce.md) around it.
        Taking time to do things right.
        Repeating the near successes to see if I can improve on them.

        Took gaining enough confidence that I wouldn’t ruin the dinner to start working on more of these things.
        Some of that came from having a second plan, or from knowing more about why something wasn’t working, and trying to do it a second time.

        Lots of little disasters, and a few big ones up until this point (March 2025).
        - Lasagne was very borning
        - Lots of sauces not saucing
        - Over salty carbonara
        - Weird croque monsieur
        - Very sweet and not great tikka masala
        - Over cooked chicken breast for a [chicken rice bowl](recipes/chicken-rice-bowl.md)

        The why behind different things has helped a lot for fast improvement e.g.
        - What salt does to proteins
        - How gluten works
        - The components that go into the experience of food, and thinking these through in planning and preparing
