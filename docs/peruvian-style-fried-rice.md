
# Peruvian-Style Fried Rice (Arroz Chaufa) with Carrot and Corn

**Serves:** 2

??? note "Ingredients"

    ??? info "Rice"

        - ¾ cup basmati rice *(texture, base)*
        - 1½ cups chicken stock or water *(flavor, cooking liquid)*

    ??? info "Main Components"

        - 3 eggs, scrambled and roughly chopped *(richness, texture)*
        - 1 medium carrot, finely diced *(sweetness, color, texture)*
        - ½ cup frozen corn kernels *(sweetness, texture, color)*

    ??? info "Aromatics"

        - ¼ cup red onion, finely diced *(pungency, sweetness when cooked)*
        - 1 clove garlic, minced *(flavor base)*
        - 1 tsp fresh ginger, minced *(warmth, spice)*
        - ¼ red capsicum, finely diced *(sweetness, color)*
        - 2 spring onions, thinly sliced (separate white and green parts) *(freshness, mild onion flavor)*

    ??? info "Sauce Mix"

        - 1½ tbsp soy sauce *(umami, saltiness)*
        - ¼ tsp sesame oil *(nutty aroma)*
        - Pinch of ground cumin *(earthy warmth)*
        - Pinch of sugar *(balances saltiness)*

    ??? info "For Cooking"

        - 2 tbsp vegetable oil *(fat for cooking)*
        - Salt and pepper to taste *(seasoning)*

??? note "Preparation"

    ??? info "Rice Preparation (Start First)"

        1. Rinse basmati rice until water runs clear. Cook rice in chicken stock using absorption method - bring to boil, reduce heat to lowest setting, cover and cook for 12 minutes. Let stand 5 minutes, then fluff. Spread rice on your large wooden chopping board to cool while you prep everything else.

    ??? info "Prep Work"

        1. Mix all sauce ingredients in a small bowl and set aside.

        2. Scramble eggs in the small nonstick pan over medium-low heat until just set. Remove, cool, and roughly chop. Set aside.

        3. Dice all vegetables uniformly (5mm pieces work well).

??? note "Method"

    ??? info "Cooking (Use Your Wok)"

        1. Heat wok over high heat until smoking. Add oil and swirl to coat.

        2. Add carrots first and stir-fry for 1-2 minutes. Add red onion and capsicum, stir-fry for 2-3 minutes until softened.

        3. Add garlic, fresh ginger, and white parts of spring onions, stir-fry for 30 seconds until fragrant.

        4. Add corn and stir-fry for 1 minute.

        5. Add all the cooled rice, breaking up any clumps with your wok spatula. Stir-fry for 2-3 minutes, keeping everything moving.

        6. Pour sauce mixture over rice and toss thoroughly. Add chopped eggs and green parts of spring onions. Toss and season with salt and pepper.

??? note "Notes"

    ??? info "Suggestions for Enhancement"

        - **Higher Heat:** Keep that wok screaming hot for proper wok hei - that smoky flavor you get from high-heat cooking.
        - **Finishing Touch:** Add a squeeze of lime juice just before serving.

    ??? info "Storage and Leftover Tips"

        - Store leftovers in airtight containers for up to 3 days in refrigerator.
        - Reheat in the wok over high heat with a splash of stock.

    ??? info "Notes on Techniques, Ingredients, and Plating"

        #### Technique Notes

        - Fresh rice will work fine - just keep it moving in the wok and don't overcook.
        - Have everything prepped and ready - wok cooking moves fast.

        #### Plating Presentation

        - Serve in warmed bowls.
        - Garnish with extra sliced spring onions and a wedge of lime.
        - Serve your prepared chicken alongside or mixed through just before plating.
