# Mexican Chipotle Chicken

??? note "Ingredients"

    - **1 kg chicken thighs or breasts** (protein) - thighs cut into 3 pieces each, breasts sliced against the grain into 3-5 cm strips
    - **100g can chipotles in adobo**, diced/minced (smoky heat, umami, acidity)
    - **1 tbsp kosher salt** (seasoning, moisture retention)
    - **1 tbsp smoked paprika** (smoky flavor, color)
    - **1 tbsp cumin** (earthy warmth)
    - **2 tsp chilli powder** (heat, depth)
    - **1-2 tsp minced garlic** or 2-3 cloves, minced (aromatic, savory)
    - **Up to ¼ cup neutral oil** (optional - helps paste consistency and browning along with texture and flavor - but left out for health as it’s benefits aren’t relevant to the uses I have for the chicken)
    - **Lime or lemon juice** (brightness, acidity) - for serving

??? note "Method"

    ??? info "Prep"

        1. Cut chicken into specified pieces

        2. Mince chipotles in adobo and garlic

        3. Combine chipotles, salt, smoked paprika, cumin, chilli powder, and garlic in a bowl to form a paste (add oil only if paste is too dry)

    ??? info "Marinate"

        1. Add chicken to bowl and mix thoroughly to coat all pieces

        2. Marinate for minimum 1 hour, ideally 24 hours, up to 3 days in refrigerator

    ??? info "Cook"

        1. Preheat oven on grill setting to 230°C

        2. Arrange chicken on baking tray

        3. Cook for 14-16 minutes until thickest piece reaches safe internal temperature (75°C)

        4. Rest for 5 minutes

        5. Cut into smaller pieces using kitchen shears

    ??? info "Serve"

        - If eating immediately, squeeze lime or lemon juice over chicken
        - Otherwise, serve with accompaniments that provide citrus tang

??? note "Notes"

    - The chipotles in adobo provide the backbone of flavor - smoky, spicy, and slightly sweet
    - Marinating for 24 hours allows the salt to penetrate and tenderize the meat while the spices develop deeper flavor
    - High heat grilling creates caramelization and char for enhanced flavor and texture
    - Citrus is essential to balance the richness and heat - consider serving with lime wedges, cilantro-lime rice, or a citrus-based slaw
