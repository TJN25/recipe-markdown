# Satay Noodles

(1-2 servings)
Sauce Mix
- 2 tbsp soy sauce (umami base)
- 1 tbsp smooth peanut butter (satay flavor, richness)
- 1.5 tsp brown sugar (balance, sweetness)
- 1 tbsp white vinegar (clean acid)
- ¼ chicken stock cube in 2 tbsp very hot water (depth)
Aromatics & Heat
- 3 cloves garlic, minced (base flavor) OR 2-3 tsp minced garlic
- 1cm ginger, finely grated (warmth, complexity)
- 2-3 bird's eye chilis, finely chopped (fresh heat) OR 1.5 tsp chilli powder
- ½ onion, finely diced (sweetness)
- 2 spring onions, whites and greens separated (layered flavor)
Core Ingredients
- Egg noodles (1-2 servings)
- 1 egg
- 2 tbsp oil for cooking
Prep Stage
1. Sauce Prep
- Dissolve stock cube in very hot water
- Immediately whisk in peanut butter until completely smooth
- Gradually mix in soy sauce, sugar, and vinegar
- Taste for balance
- Keep warm if possible
2. Aromatics Prep
- Microplane ginger (keep juice)
- Mince garlic
- Chop chilis (small plastic board)
- Dice onion
- Slice spring onions (separate whites/greens)
3. Noodle Prep
- Cook until just underdone
- Reserve ¼ cup cooking water
- Drain, toss with tiny bit of oil
Cooking Stage (Cast Iron Pan)
1. Heat pan over medium-high until hot
2. Add oil, wait until shimmering
3. Add garlic, ginger, chilis, onion, spring onion whites
4. Cook until onions are translucent and edges start to brown (3-4 mins)
- Watch for light golden color
- Pull pan off heat if cooking too fast
- Garlic should be fragrant but not dark
5. Push ingredients to side, add egg
- Break up immediately when whites start turning opaque (about 30 seconds)
- Keep pieces small
- Mix with aromatics
6. Add noodles and sauce
- Keep heat medium-high
- Toss continuously
- Sauce should coat evenly
Final Adjustments

    ??? info "Taste and assess"
        - If lacking depth: add ½ tsp Worcestershire sauce
        - If needs more heat: add extra fresh chili
        - If sauce separating: remove from heat, stir vigorously
        - If too thick: add reserved noodle water gradually
        - If too dry: combine 1 tbsp noodle water with:
        - ½ tbsp soy sauce
        - Tiny pinch sugar
        - If flavors muted: add small splash vinegar
        Finish
        - Turn off heat
        - Garnish with spring onion greens
        - Serve immediately

??? note "Technical Notes"
    - Keep sauce warm and well-mixed
    - Watch aromatics carefully for proper browning
    - Egg should be broken up while still very wet
    - Final sauce should be glossy and cling to noodles

??? note "Storage"
    - Best eaten fresh
    - Store aromatics separately if prepping ahead
    - Freeze unused chilis
    - Keep leftover ginger wrapped in fridge
