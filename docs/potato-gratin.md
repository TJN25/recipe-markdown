# Potato Gratin

??? note "Ingredients"
    - 4-5 medium potatoes, thinly sliced (unpeeled) using a mandoline
    - 1 medium onion, thinly sliced
    - 2 tbsp butter, melted
    - 1½ cups full-fat milk
    - ½ cup Greek yogurt
    - 1 tbsp minced garlic
    - ¼ tsp nutmeg
    - 2 tsp sea salt flakes
    - 1 tsp chicken stock powder
    - 2 sprigs thyme, leaves picked
    - 1 cup shredded cheddar and colby cheese mix

??? note "Preparation Steps"
    Prep Work (20 minutes)
    1. Preheat oven to 180°C fan-forced (or 200°C conventional).
    2. Thinly slice onion using mandoline or sharp knife.
    3. Par-boil potato slices:
    - Bring a large pot of water to a gentle simmer (not rolling boil)
    - Add potato slices in batches for just 3-4 minutes until slightly softened but not falling apart
    - Remove with a slotted spoon to a clean kitchen towel and pat dry
    Assembly (15 minutes)
    1. In a medium baking dish, arrange some of the par-boiled potato slices vertically.
    2. Scatter some thinly sliced onions between potato sections.
    3. Continue alternating potato slices and onion until dish is filled.
    4. In a bowl, whisk together melted butter, milk, Greek yogurt, minced garlic, nutmeg, salt, and chicken stock powder until well combined.
    5. Pour the milk mixture evenly over the potatoes and onions.
    6. Sprinkle with thyme leaves, followed by the cheese mixture.

??? note "Cooking"

    1 hour 30 minutes
    1. Cover with baking paper (to prevent cheese sticking), then foil.
    2. Bake for 1 hour at 180°C fan-forced.
    3. Remove foil and baking paper, then grill for 10-15 minutes until golden and crispy on top.
    4. Test doneness by inserting a fork - potatoes should be completely tender.
    Ingredient Purpose Notes
    - Potatoes: Main ingredient, provides starch and structure (leaving skins on adds nutrition and texture)
    - Onions: Adds sweetness and depth of flavor when cooked (becomes mellow and aromatic)
    - Butter: Adds richness and fat for flavor development
    - Milk: Creates creamy sauce base
    - Greek yogurt: Adds tanginess and creaminess (contains proteins that help thicken the sauce)
    - Garlic: Provides aromatic flavor base
    - Nutmeg: Adds warm, nutty complexity that enhances creamy dishes
    - Salt: Enhances all flavors (added after par-boiling to prevent toughening)
    - Chicken stock powder: Adds savory depth (umami)
    - Thyme: Aromatic herb that pairs well with potatoes
    - Cheese mix: Creates golden crust, adds flavor and richness
    Technique Notes
    Onion Integration Tips
    - Slice onions very thinly (almost transparent) so they cook completely
    - Distribute them evenly throughout the dish rather than in concentrated layers
    - The onions will become sweet and mellow during cooking, complementing the potatoes
    Greek Yogurt Benefits
    - The tanginess balances the richness of the dish
    - Proteins in yogurt help create a thicker, more stable sauce
    - Adds creaminess with less fat than using all cream
    Nutmeg Notes
    - Freshly grated nutmeg is best if available
    - The nutmeg works with dairy to create a classic flavor profile
    - Just ¼ teaspoon is enough - it should be a background note, not dominant
    Plating and Serving
    - Let stand for 10 minutes before serving to set the sauce
    - Serve with a sprinkle of fresh thyme leaves
    - A simple side salad with vinaigrette provides acidic balance to the rich gratin

??? note "Storage"
    - Refrigerate leftovers in an airtight container for up to 3 days
    - Reheat in toaster oven at 160°C until warmed through
