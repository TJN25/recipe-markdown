# Jackfruit Tacos

Salsa Recipe

??? note "Ingredients"
    - 2 fresh tomatoes, diced (juicy base)
    - 1/4 red onion, finely diced (sharp flavor)
    - 1 small fresh chili or 1/2 tsp cayenne pepper (heat)
    - 1 clove garlic, minced (aromatic)
    - 1 tbsp lime juice (acidity)
    - 1/2 tsp cumin (warmth)
    - Salt and pepper to taste (seasoning)
    - Fresh coriander if available (brightness)

    ??? info "Instructions"
        1. Dice tomatoes into small cubes and place in a bowl
        2. Finely dice red onion and add to tomatoes
        3. Mince garlic and chili, add to mixture
        4. Add lime juice, cumin, salt and pepper
        5. Mix well and let sit for 15 minutes before serving to allow flavors to meld

        Quick Jackfruit Taco Filling Recipe (400g can)

??? note "Ingredients"
    - 1 can (400g) young green jackfruit in water or brine
    - 2 tbsp olive oil
    - 1/2 medium red onion, finely diced
    - 2 cloves garlic, minced
    - 1 tsp ground cumin
    - 1/2 tsp smoked paprika
    - 1/2 tsp ground coriander
    - Small pinch ground cinnamon
    - 2-3 whole cloves, ground in mortar and pestle (or 1/8 tsp ground)
    - 1/2 tsp cayenne pepper or chili powder, to taste
    - 1 tbsp tomato paste
    - 2 tbsp soy sauce
    - 1.5 tbsp maple syrup (or honey if not strictly vegan)
    - Pinch of black pepper
    - 1/2 tsp liquid smoke (or 1/4 tsp extra smoked paprika)
    - 1 tbsp apple cider vinegar

??? note "Method"
    1. Prepare jackfruit:
    - Drain jackfruit thoroughly
    - Rinse well under cold water
    - Squeeze out as much liquid as possible with paper towels
    - Cut off firm, pointy ends (optional)
    - Pull apart into shreds using forks
    - Pat dry again
    2. Cook base:
    - Heat olive oil in cast iron pan over medium heat
    - Sauté onion until soft and lightly caramelized (5-7 minutes)
    - Add garlic, cook for 1 minute until fragrant
    - Add spices (cumin, paprika, coriander, cinnamon, ground cloves), cook for 30 seconds
    - Stir in tomato paste and cayenne/chili powder
    3. Add jackfruit and simmer:
    - Add prepared jackfruit, soy sauce, maple syrup, liquid smoke, and vinegar
    - Mix well, breaking up jackfruit pieces further
    - Simmer gently for 15-20 minutes, stirring occasionally
    - Let cool and rest for as long as possible (at least 1 hour)
    4. Final roasting:
    - Preheat oven to 220°C
    - Spread jackfruit on baking paper-lined tray
    - Bake for 25-30 minutes until edges are browned and slightly crispy
    - Stir halfway through for even browning

    Spaghetti Bolognese

??? note "Ingredients"
    - Fettuccine pasta
    - Prime beef mince
    - 1 carrot (texture, sweetness)
    - 1 onion (aromatic base, sweetness)
    - 1 capsicum (freshness, sweetness)
    - 2-3 cloves garlic, minced (aromatic, depth)
    - 1-2 tbsp tomato paste (umami, richness)
    - 1 can diced tomatoes (base, acidity)
    - 1 beef stock cube (savory depth)
    - Splash of red wine vinegar (brightness, acidity)
    - 1-2 bay leaves (aromatic complexity)
    - 1 tsp dried oregano (herbal notes)
    - 30g butter (richness, silkiness)
    - Canola oil (cooking medium)
    - Salt (seasoning)
    - White vinegar (final balancing)

??? note "Method"
    Prep Stage
    1. Dice onion, carrot, and capsicum into small, even pieces
    2. Mince garlic
    3. Measure out tomato paste
    4. Fill large saucepan with water for pasta
    Cooking Stage
    1. Brown the mince: Heat canola oil in large frypan over medium-high heat. Add beef mince and break up, cooking until well browned. Remove to a bowl.
    2. Sweat vegetables: In the same pan, add a little more oil if needed. Add onion, carrot, and capsicum. Cook on medium heat until softened (about 5-7 minutes).
    3. Build flavor base: Return mince to pan. Add garlic and cook for 1 minute until fragrant. Add tomato paste and cook for 1-2 minutes until darkened.
    4. Create sauce: Add diced tomatoes, crumbled beef stock cube, splash of red wine vinegar, bay leaves, and oregano. Stir well.
    5. Reduce: Simmer on low-medium heat until sauce reaches desired consistency (15-30 minutes).
    6. Cook pasta: While sauce reduces, add salt to pasta water (should taste like sea water) and bring to boil. Cook fettuccine until just under al dente.
    7. Combine: Using tongs, transfer pasta directly to sauce. Add 30g butter and toss everything together, allowing pasta to finish cooking in the sauce.
    8. Final adjustments: If too thick, add a small amount of plain water to reach desired consistency. Taste and adjust seasoning with salt and a splash of white vinegar if needed.
    9. Rest: Allow to sit for 2 minutes before serving.
    Enhancement Notes
    - Remove bay leaves before serving
    - For deeper flavor, consider adding a dash of Worcestershire sauce during the simmering stage
    - If available, substitute red wine for the vinegar (use about 1/4 cup and reduce before adding tomatoes)
    - A sprinkle of parmesan cheese just before serving adds excellent finishing flavor
    Plating Suggestion
    Serve in warmed bowls with a small amount of freshly chopped parsley or basil if available. A light drizzle of olive oil on top adds a professional touch and enhances richness.

    Iskender-Style Meal: Chicken & Jackfruit Options
    Jackfruit Component (1/2 can serving)
    - 1/2 can (200g) young green jackfruit in water or brine (protein substitute)
    - 1 tablespoon olive oil (cooking medium, flavor)
    - 1/2 teaspoon smoked paprika (depth, color)
    - 1/2 teaspoon cumin (warmth, earthiness)
    - 1/4 teaspoon ground coriander (citrusy notes)
    - Pinch of cayenne pepper (heat, optional)
    - 1/2 teaspoon soy sauce or tamari (umami enhancer)
    - 1.5g salt (about 1/4 teaspoon) (flavor enhancer)
    - Black pepper to taste (warmth)
    Preparation for Jackfruit (30 minutes total)
    Prep Stage (20 minutes)
    1. Prepare jackfruit:
    - Drain jackfruit thoroughly in a colander
    - Rinse well under cold water to remove brine/can flavor
    - Using your hands, squeeze out as much liquid as possible
    - Place jackfruit on paper towels and press firmly to remove remaining moisture
    - Pull apart into shreds using two forks or your fingers
    - Pat dry again with paper towels (crucial for browning)
    2. Prepare marinade:
    - In a bowl, mix olive oil, smoked paprika, cumin, coriander, cayenne, soy sauce, salt and pepper
    - Toss jackfruit in the marinade, ensuring all pieces are well coated
    - Let marinate for at least 15 minutes (longer if possible)
    Cooking Stage (10 minutes)
    1. Cook jackfruit:
    - Heat cast iron pan over medium-high heat until very hot
    - Add a small amount of oil if needed
    - Spread jackfruit in a single layer (don't overcrowd)
    - Let cook undisturbed for 3-4 minutes until edges begin to crisp
    - Toss and continue cooking for another 2-3 minutes until golden and slightly crispy
    - Remove from heat

    ??? info "Set up a station with"

        - Chicken (prepared according to original recipe)
        - Jackfruit option
        - Spiced couscous
        - Fresh vegetables (lettuce, spinach, tomato, cucumber, red onion)

        - Coconut yogurt (for vegan option)
        - Fresh herbs for garnish
        Notes for Success
        - The key to good jackfruit texture is thorough drying before cooking
        - Jackfruit benefits from slightly higher heat than chicken to develop crispy edges
        - Label sauces clearly if you're serving both vegan and non-vegan options
        - The jackfruit will have a slightly sweeter, more tangy profile than the chicken
        This approach gives everyone options while minimizing waste by testing a smaller portion of jackfruit.
