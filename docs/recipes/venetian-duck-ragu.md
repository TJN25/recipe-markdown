# Venetian Duck Ragu

??? note "Ingredients"

    for 2-3 servings
    - 2 duck legs (protein, main flavor)
    - 1 tbsp olive oil (fat, cooking medium)
    - 1 onion, finely chopped (aromatics, flavor base)
    - 2 garlic cloves, crushed (aromatics, depth of flavor)
    - 1 tsp ground cinnamon (warmth, complexity)
    - 1 whole clove (aromatic spice, warm notes)
    - 1 tsp plain flour (thickener)
    - 125ml Pinot Noir (acidity, depth, deglazing)
    - 1 can baby Roma tomatoes (acidity, sweetness, body)
    - 1 chicken stock cube made up to 125ml (savory base, umami)
    - 2 strips orange zest (brightness, aromatic complexity)
    - 1 tsp dried rosemary (aromatic, complements duck)
    - 1 bay leaf (subtle herbal notes)
    - 2 tsp sugar (balances acidity)
    - 2 tsp balsamic vinegar (brightness, depth, balances richness)
    - 1 tbsp milk (enriches, smooths flavor)
    - 30g butter (enriches, smooths flavor)
    - Salt and pepper to taste (seasoning)
    - Parmesan, grated, to serve (umami, salt)
    - Fresh parsley, chopped, for garnish (brightness, color)
    Preparation and Cooking Instructions
    Prep Stage (15 minutes)
    1. Finely chop the onion and crush the garlic cloves
    2. Use microplane to remove 2 strips of orange zest (avoid white pith)
    3. Prepare chicken stock with stock cube
    4. Chop some fresh parsley for garnish
    Cooking the Ragu (2 hours)
    1. Heat olive oil in cast iron pan over medium heat
    2. Season duck legs with salt and pepper, then brown on all sides for 8-10 minutes until golden. Remove to a plate
    3. Add onions to the same pan and cook for 5 minutes until softened
    4. Add garlic and cook for another minute
    5. Add the whole clove and toast for 30 seconds
    6. Stir in cinnamon and flour, cook for 1 minute (this creates a light roux)
    7. Return duck to the pan, add wine and let it bubble for 2 minutes to reduce slightly
    8. Add tomatoes, stock, orange zest, dried rosemary, bay leaf, sugar, and seasoning
    9. Bring to a simmer, then lower heat, cover with a lid and cook for 1.5 hours, stirring occasionally
    Final Stage (30 minutes)
    1. Remove duck legs from the sauce and place on a plate
    2. Pull off and discard the fat, then shred the meat with two forks and discard the bones
    3. Remove and discard the bay leaf, orange zest, and clove from the sauce
    4. Return the meat to the sauce with the milk and simmer, uncovered, for 10-15 minutes to reduce slightly
    5. Stir in the balsamic vinegar in the last 2 minutes of cooking
    6. Taste and adjust seasoning if needed
    7. Serve the ragu over the mashed potatoes with grated Parmesan and fresh parsley
