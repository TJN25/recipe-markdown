# Quick Beef Pide

??? note "Ingredients"

    - 100g Greek yoghurt
    - 75g self-raising flour
    - 50g feta
    - 0.5 brown onion
    - 1 garlic clove
    - 1 tsp dried marjoram
    - 1-2 tsp sumac
    - 1/4-1/2 tsp cinnamon
    - 0.5 tbsp olive oil
    - 1.5-2 tbsp tomato paste
    - 1 tsp ground cumin
    - 0.5 tsp ground coriander
    - 0.5 tsp smoked paprika
    - 1 tsp beef stock powder
    - 1/4-1/2 tsp MSG (optional)
    - 125g beef mince
    - Salt and pepper

??? note "Method"

    1. Preheat oven to 220°C.

    2. Mix 75g Greek yoghurt, 75g self-raising flour, and a pinch of salt in a bowl until dough forms. Turn onto floured surface.

    3. Knead dough for 2 minutes until smooth. Transfer back to bowl and let rest.

    4. Finely dice ½ onion and mince 1 garlic clove.

    5. Heat medium frying pan over medium heat. Add ½ tbsp olive oil.

    6. Add onions, garlic, 1.5-2 tbsp tomato paste, 1 tsp ground cumin, ½ tsp ground coriander, ½ tsp smoked paprika, ¼-½ tsp cinnamon, 1 tsp marjoram, 1 tsp beef stock powder, MSG (if using), and pinch of salt. Cook 8-10 minutes until softened and golden.

    7. Add 125g beef mince and break apart with spoon. Cook 5 minutes.

    8. Add 1-2 tsp sumac to beef mixture. Season with salt and pepper. Remove from heat and let cool.

    9. Divide dough in half. Roll each half into large rectangles on parchment paper.

    10. Spread beef mixture over middle of each rectangle. Fold edges over by 2cm. Pinch and twist ends to create boat shape.

    11. Transfer pides to baking sheet. Bake 10-12 minutes until golden brown.

    12. Mix remaining 25g yoghurt and 50g feta. Blitz with hand blender until smooth. Season with salt and pepper.

    13. Top pide with feta yoghurt mixture.
