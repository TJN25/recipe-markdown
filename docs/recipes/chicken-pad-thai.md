# Chicken Pad Thai

1 hour

**Serves:** 4

??? note "Ingredients"

    ??? info "Main Components"

        - 225g flat rice noodles (base carbohydrate, texture)
        - 1 large chicken breast (250g), pounded to ½" thickness, bite-sized pieces (protein)
        - 2 eggs (protein, richness, binding)
        - Salt and pepper to taste (seasoning)

    ??? info "Vegetables & Garnishes"

        - 1 cup fresh bean sprouts (crunch, freshness)
        - 3-4 stalks spring onions, sliced (fresh aromatics)
        - ¼ medium white onion, sliced (savory base)
        - 30g peanuts for garnish, optional (crunch, nutty flavor)

    ??? info "Sauce"

        - ¼ cup ketchup (sweet-tangy base)
        - 2 tbsp fish sauce (umami, salt)
        - 2 tbsp sugar (balance, caramelization)
        - 1 tsp minced garlic (2 cloves) (aromatic base)
        - 1 tsp peanut butter (richness, nutty depth)
        - 1 lime, juiced (bright acidity)
        - ½ tsp crushed red pepper flakes (heat)
        - 1 tsp soy sauce (umami, salt)

??? note "Method"

    ??? info "Prep Phase"

        - Cook noodles to al dente according to package
        - Season chicken with salt and pepper
        - Whisk together all sauce ingredients

    ??? info "Cook Phase"

        - Cook chicken in wok over medium heat (6-8 minutes)
        - Remove chicken, set aside
        - In same pan, fry eggs (1 minute)
        - Break up eggs, add white onions and sprouts
        - Return chicken to pan, add sauce
        - Add noodles, toss to coat
        - Stir in green onions and cilantro

    ??? info "Serve"

        - Garnish with peanuts if desired
        - Serve immediately while hot

??? note "Notes"
    - For extra heat: Add 1-2 tsp sriracha
    - Protein alternatives: Shrimp, tofu, or steak work well
    - Make ahead: Prep sauce and cut chicken in advance
    - Storage: Best eaten fresh, keeps 2-3 days refrigerated
