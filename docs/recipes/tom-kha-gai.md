# Authentic Tom Kha Gai

Thai coconut chicken soup

Serves: 4
Prep: 15 minutes (I don’t believe this)
Cook: 25 minutes (I don’t believe this)

??? note "Ingredients"
    - 2 cups chicken stock, unsalted, preferably homemade
    - 1 1/2 cups coconut milk
    - 1/2 teaspoon table salt
    - 1 lb chicken thigh, boneless skinless, cut into 1-inch pieces
    - 1 stalk lemongrass, bottom half only, smashed and cut into 2-inch pieces
    - 12 thin slices galangal (ginger as I can’t get galangal)
    - 5 makrut lime leaves, twisted to bruise and torn into big chunks (lime zest if it’s not accessible)
    - 1-3 Thai chilies, to taste, smashed until broken or cut into large pieces (more when cooking just for me)
    - 2 tablespoons fish sauce
    - 1 teaspoon finely chopped palm sugar, or granulated sugar
    - 5.5 oz oyster mushrooms, torn into bite-size pieces (portabello if nothing else is available)
    - 2 1/2 tablespoons lime juice
    - chopped green onion and/or cilantro, for garnish
    - jasmine rice, for serving (optional) (basmati to avoid even more rices at home)

??? note "Method"
    1. Bring the chicken stock to a boil, then add the salt and chicken thigh. Simmer gently for 15 minutes or until the chicken is fork tender.
    2. Add the coconut milk, lemongrass, galangal, makrut lime leaves, chilies, mushrooms, about half of the fish sauce, and sugar. Simmer for 5 minutes to infuse.
    3. Taste and add more fish sauce as needed. Turn off the heat and add about 2 tablespoons of the lime juice, then taste and add more as needed. Top with green onions and/or cilantro.
    4. Serve with jasmine rice as part of a larger Thai meal, or enjoy on its own.

??? note "Notes"
    - For a lighter soup, reduce the coconut milk by about 1/2 cup and increase the chicken stock by the same amount.
    - If using chicken breast, cut into 1/2-inch thick slices and marinate in 1 tablespoon of fish sauce while prepping. Skip step 1, omit the salt, reduce the chicken stock to 1 1/2 cups and add it with the coconut milk. Add the chicken breast with the mushrooms and cook for a couple of minutes just until done. Taste and adjust the salt at the end.
    - The lemongrass, galangal and makrut lime leaves are for infusion only and are not meant to be eaten. Remove before serving or warn people.
