# Doner Kebab

**Serves:** 4

??? note "Ingredients"

    - **500g beef mince** (protein, fat, texture) - use regular beef mince from Woolworths
    - **½ large onion, grated** (moisture, sweetness, helps bind)
    - **1½ garlic cloves, minced** (aromatic, flavour)
    - **2 tablespoons plain unsweetened yoghurt** (tenderiser, moisture, helps bind)
    - **1 teaspoon ground cumin** (earthy, warm spice)
    - **1 teaspoon ground coriander** (citrusy, aromatic)
    - **½ teaspoon paprika** (colour, mild sweetness)
    - **½ teaspoon dried oregano** (herbal, Mediterranean flavour)
    - **½ teaspoon salt** (salty, enhances flavours)
    - **¼ teaspoon black pepper** (spice, heat)

??? note "Equipment Needed"

    - Large metal bowl
    - Box grater
    - Baking paper
    - Small baking tray (for toaster oven) or large conventional oven with baking tray
    - Small bowl with water
    - Chef knife
    - Chopping board

??? note "Preparation"

    ??? info "Prep ingredients"

        - Peel and grate half an onion using the box grater over a chopping board
        - Mince 1½ garlic cloves finely with chef knife
        - Measure out yoghurt and spices into small ramekins or directly into mixing bowl

    ??? info "Mix the meat"

        - In large metal bowl, combine beef mince, grated onion, minced garlic, yoghurt, cumin, coriander, paprika, oregano, salt, and pepper
        - Mix thoroughly with your hands until very well combined (this helps bind the meat and distribute flavours evenly)

??? note "Method"

    ??? info "Preheat oven"

        - For toaster oven: Set to 200°C with convection setting
        - For conventional oven: Preheat to 200°C with fan bake

    ??? info "Shape the meat"

        - Cut one sheet of baking paper approximately 30cm square
        - Place all the meat mixture in the centre of the baking paper
        - Keep a small bowl of water nearby
        - Dip your fingers in water to prevent sticking
        - Press and shape the meat into a thin, even rectangle (approximately 20cm x 25cm)

    ??? info "Roll the meat"

        - Starting from the long side, fold the baking paper over the meat
        - Roll tightly until it forms a flat, compact log
        - Twist the ends of the paper to secure

    ??? info "Initial bake"

        - Place the baking paper-wrapped log onto your baking tray
        - Bake for 15 minutes

    ??? info "Break apart and crisp"

        - Remove from oven and let cool for 2-3 minutes until you can handle it
        - Carefully unroll the baking paper
        - Break the meat apart into small chunks or slice thinly with your chef knife
        - Spread the pieces out on the baking tray

    ??? info "Finish under grill"

        - Switch toaster oven to grill setting (or conventional oven to grill)
        - Grill for 2-3 minutes until lightly golden with crisp edges
        - Watch carefully to avoid burning

??? note "Notes"

    ??? info "Serving Suggestions"

        - **In pita bread:** Warm pita pockets or flatbreads, fill with the meat, shredded lettuce, diced tomatoes, sliced red onion, pickles, and drizzle with tahini sauce or garlic sauce
        - **On rice:** Serve over rice with a side salad
        - **Wrap style:** Use Lebanese bread or tortillas with your choice of salad and sauce

??? note "Storage"

    - Store leftover cooked meat in an airtight container in the fridge for up to 3 days
    - Reheat in the toaster oven at 180°C for 5-7 minutes until warmed through

??? note "Notes"

    ??? info "Notes on Techniques and Ingredients"

        - **Grating the onion:** This releases moisture and helps bind the meat while adding flavour without chunks. Squeeze out excess liquid if the mixture seems too wet.
        - **Yoghurt as tenderiser:** The acidity in yoghurt helps break down proteins, making the meat more tender. Greek yoghurt works well too.
        - **Rolling technique:** Rolling the meat tightly in baking paper mimics the compressed texture of traditional döner kebab cooked on a vertical spit.
        - **Breaking apart:** This step creates more surface area for crisping and browning, giving you those delicious crispy edges.
        - **Spice variations:** Add ½ teaspoon chilli flakes for heat, or ½ teaspoon sumac for tanginess.
        - **Make ahead:** You can shape and roll the meat, then refrigerate for up to 24 hours before baking.
