# Slow Cooker Ketchup

**Makes:** about 2 cups

??? note "Ingredients"
    - 1 can (400g) diced tomatoes (base, umami)
    - 1/4 cup white vinegar (acidity, preservation)
    - 3 tbsp brown sugar (sweetness, depth from molasses)
    - 1/2 onion, finely diced (savory base, natural sweetness)
    - 1 large garlic clove, minced (depth, aromatic)
    - 1/2 tsp salt (seasoning, enhances flavors)
    - 1/8 tsp ground cloves (warmth, traditional ketchup note)
    - 1/4 tsp ground coriander (citrus notes, brightness)
    - Optional: pinch of ground black pepper (warmth, depth)

    ??? info "Enhanced Instructions"

    ??? info "Prep"
        1. Finely dice onion using chef knife
        2. Mince garlic with microplane or knife
        3. Measure all other ingredients into separate ramekins

??? note "Cooking"
    1. Combine all ingredients in slow cooker
    2. Mix well with wooden spoon
    3. Cook on LOW for 8 hours with lid on
    4. After 8 hours, blend until smooth using stick blender
    5. Switch to HIGH, remove lid
    6. Continue cooking 1-2 hours until desired thickness, stirring occasionally
    7. Test consistency by running spoon through mixture - it should leave a clear path that slowly fills in
    8. Cool completely before transferring to airtight container

    ??? info "Enhancement Suggestions"
        - Add 1 tbsp tomato paste (deepens color and intensifies tomato flavor)
        - Include 1/4 tsp smoked paprika for subtle smokiness
        - Add 1/8 tsp celery salt for classic ketchup complexity
        - Consider adding 1 tbsp Worcestershire sauce for umami depth

??? note "Technical Notes"
    - Slow cooking develops deeper flavors through caramelization
    - Blending while hot ensures smoothest texture
    - Final reduction concentrates flavors and achieves proper consistency
    - Proper thickness is crucial - too thin won't cling to food, too thick becomes paste-like
    - Will thicken slightly more upon cooling

??? note "Storage"
    - Keep refrigerated in airtight container
    - Best used within 3-4 weeks
    - Can be frozen in small portions for longer storage

    ??? info "Usage Ideas"
        - Classic condiment for burgers/fries
        - Base for BBQ sauce
        - Add to meatloaf mixture
        - Blend with mayo for special sauce

??? note "Notes"
    Not sweet enough to want to use for a lot of normal ketchup uses
