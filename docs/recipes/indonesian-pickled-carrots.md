# Indonesian Oil-Based Pickled Carrots (Acar Wortel)

**Makes:** ~1 cup

??? note "Ingredients"

    ??? info "Main Ingredients"

        - 1 large carrot (~125g), peeled and julienned (main vegetable, sweet, texture)

    ??? info "Pickling Oil Base"

        - 60ml neutral oil (vegetable/canola) (fat, carries flavors, preserves)
        - 40ml white vinegar (acidity, preserves, balances)
        - 1 tbsp sugar (sweetness, balances acidity)
        - 1/2 tsp salt (salty, enhances flavors, preserves)

    ??? info "Aromatics"

        - 1-2 cloves garlic, thinly sliced (pungent, aromatic)
        - 1 shallot, thinly sliced (sweet, aromatic)
        - 1cm fresh ginger, julienned (warmth, spice)
        - 1cm fresh turmeric, julienned OR 1/2 tsp ground turmeric (earthy, color, anti-inflammatory)
        - 1-2 bird's eye chilies, sliced (heat, optional)

    ??? info "Ground Spices"

        - 1/2 tsp ground coriander (citrusy, aromatic)
        - 1/4 tsp mustard powder (pungent, flavor)
        - Small pinch ground cinnamon (warmth, depth)

??? note "Preparation"

    ??? info "Prep Work (10 minutes)"

        1. Peel and julienne carrot into 5cm x 3mm matchsticks using your large chef knife on the wooden chopping board

        2. Peel and thinly slice garlic and shallot

        3. Peel and julienne ginger and fresh turmeric (wear gloves for turmeric to avoid staining)

        4. Slice chilies if using

        5. Prepare a clean ramekin or small airtight container for storage

??? note "Method"

    ??? info "Quick Pickle Method (Ready in 2 hours)"

        1. In your very small saucepan, heat oil over medium-low heat (2 minutes)

        2. Add ground coriander, mustard powder, and cinnamon, stir constantly for 20 seconds until fragrant (watch carefully - ground spices burn quickly)

        3. Add shallot and garlic, cook gently until softened but not browned (2-3 minutes), stirring frequently

        4. Add ginger and turmeric (if using fresh), stir for 1 minute until fragrant

        5. If using ground turmeric, add it now with the vinegar in the next step

        6. Add carrots, stir to coat in oil and spices (1-2 minutes)

        7. Add vinegar, sugar, and salt, stir to dissolve sugar

        8. Bring to a gentle simmer, cook for 3-4 minutes until carrots are slightly softened but still crisp

        9. Add chilies in the last minute

        10. Remove from heat, let cool to room temperature

        11. Transfer to ramekin or small container with all the oil and aromatics

        12. Refrigerate for at least 2 hours before serving (flavors develop)

??? note "Notes"

    ??? info "Serving Suggestions"

        - Serve as a condiment with nasi goreng, satay, or grilled meats
        - Accompaniment to Indonesian fried chicken (ayam goreng)
        - Side dish for rice bowls or curries
        - On sandwiches or with cheese boards

??? note "Storage"

    - Store in airtight container in refrigerator
    - Keeps for 1-2 weeks if carrot remains submerged in oil
    - Always use clean utensils to remove pickles to prevent contamination

??? note "Notes"

    ??? info "Enhancement Suggestions"

        - **Flavor Variations:**
        - Add 1/2 tsp palm sugar instead of white sugar for deeper caramel notes
        - Add pinch of ground white pepper for extra heat
        - Add 1/4 tsp ground cumin for earthier flavor

        - **Technique Tips:**

        - Don't overcook carrot - it should retain crunch
        - Ground spices burn easily - keep heat low and stir constantly when blooming
        - Add ground turmeric with liquid (vinegar) rather than in hot oil to prevent bitterness
        - The oil should just cover the vegetables; this small batch should work well in a ramekin

    ??? info "Notes on Techniques and Ingredients"

        #### Ground Spices

        Ground spices bloom faster than whole spices and burn more easily. Keep heat at medium-low and stir constantly during the 20-second blooming period. The spices should smell fragrant and toasted, not acrid or burnt.

        #### Fresh vs Ground Turmeric

        Fresh turmeric provides brighter color and more complex flavor. If using ground, add it with the vinegar (step 7) rather than in the hot oil to prevent burning and bitterness. Ground turmeric is more concentrated - 1/2 tsp ground ≈ 1cm fresh.

        #### Small Batch Considerations

        This halved recipe works perfectly. The small volume actually allows for quicker flavor development. Use your very small saucepan - the reduced surface area helps maintain oil temperature and prevents evaporation.

        #### Oil Temperature

        With ground spices, temperature control is critical. If oil starts smoking, remove from heat immediately. The garlic and shallot should soften and become translucent over 2-3 minutes, not brown.

        #### Julienne Technique

        For uniform matchsticks, cut carrot into 5cm lengths, slice lengthwise into 3mm planks, stack planks and slice into 3mm strips. Uniform size ensures even pickling.

        #### Storage Container

        A ramekin works well for this small batch. Ensure carrot is submerged in oil - press down gently with a clean spoon if needed. If carrot floats above oil line, it may spoil faster.

        #### Plating Presentation

        Serve in a small ramekin or on a small plate alongside main dish. Drain excess oil before plating for cleaner presentation. The bright yellow-orange color contrasts beautifully with brown grilled meats or white rice. A few julienned pieces arranged in a small mound with one chili slice on top creates an appealing visual.

        # Indonesian Oil-Based Pickled Carrots (Acar Wortel) - Indomie Mi Goreng Style

        **Makes:** ~1 cup

??? note "Ingredients"

    ??? info "Main Ingredients"

        - 1 large carrot (~125g), peeled and julienned

    ??? info "Pickling Oil Base"

        - 60ml neutral oil (vegetable/canola)
        - 40ml white vinegar
        - 2 tsp kecap manis (Indonesian sweet soy sauce)
        - 1/2 tsp Kikkoman soy sauce
        - 1.5 tbsp sugar
        - 1/2 tsp salt
        - 1/4 tsp MSG (or 1/2 tsp mushroom powder)
        - 1/2 tsp fish sauce (optional)

    ??? info "Aromatics (Powdered)"

        - 1/2 tsp garlic powder
        - 1/2 tsp onion powder
        - 1/4 tsp ginger powder
        - 2-3 bird's eye chilies, sliced

    ??? info "Ground Spices"

        - 1/4 tsp white pepper
        - 1/4 tsp black pepper, freshly ground
        - 1/2 tsp ground coriander
        - 1/4 tsp smoked paprika
        - 1/4 tsp mustard powder
        - Small pinch five-spice powder
        - Tiny pinch ground turmeric (1/8 tsp, optional)

??? note "Preparation"

    ??? info "Prep Work (5 minutes)"

        1. Peel and julienne carrot into 5cm x 3mm matchsticks

        2. Slice chilies

        3. Prepare a clean ramekin or small airtight container for storage

??? note "Method"

    ??? info "Cooking Instructions (Ready in 2 hours)"

        1. In your very small saucepan, heat oil over medium-low heat (2 minutes)

        2. Add all ground spices (white pepper, black pepper, coriander, smoked paprika, mustard powder, five-spice, turmeric if using) - stir constantly 20 seconds until fragrant

        3. Add garlic powder, onion powder, ginger powder - stir 10 seconds

        4. Add carrots, stir to coat (1-2 minutes)

        5. Add vinegar, kecap manis, Kikkoman, sugar, salt, MSG, fish sauce (if using) - stir to dissolve

        6. Simmer 3-4 minutes until carrots slightly softened but still crisp

        7. Add chilies - cook 1 minute

        8. Remove from heat, cool to room temperature

        9. Transfer to container, refrigerate minimum 2 hours

??? note "Notes"

    ??? info "Serving Suggestions"

        - Condiment with nasi goreng, satay, or grilled meats
        - Side dish for rice bowls or curries

??? note "Storage"

    - Refrigerate in airtight container, keeps 1-2 weeks submerged in oil
