# Malaysian Chicken Satay Curry

**Serves:** 2

??? note "Ingredients"

    ??? info "Shopping List"

        - 500g boneless chicken, cut into pieces
        - 250ml coconut milk or cream
        - 2 tbsp peanut butter
        - 100g roasted unsalted peanuts
        - 3-4 fresh red chillies
        - 1 lime

    ??? info "Satay Seasoning"

        - 1½ tsp ground coriander *(earthy)*
        - 1½ tsp cumin *(nutty)*
        - 1½ tsp turmeric *(color)*
        - 1½ tsp paprika *(sweet)*
        - 1¼ tsp cayenne pepper *(heat)*
        - 1¼ tsp salt *(enhancer)*
        - 2 tsp raw sugar *(balance)*

    ??? info "Other Ingredients"

        - 1 onion
        - 3 garlic cloves
        - 2 tbsp canola oil
        - 2 tbsp soy sauce *(for kecap manis)*
        - 2 tbsp brown sugar *(for kecap manis)*
        - 1 tsp soy sauce *(for sauce)*
        - 1 chicken stock cube + ¾ cup water
        - 1 bay leaf *(aromatic depth)*
        - ½ tsp MSG *(umami boost)*

??? note "Method"

    ??? info "Prep (20 minutes)"

        1. Mix all satay seasoning ingredients in bowl

        2. Cut chicken into bite-sized pieces, marinate with 2½ tbsp seasoning + ⅓ grated onion (minimum 20 minutes)

        3. Make kecap manis: Simmer 2 tbsp soy sauce + 2 tbsp brown sugar in small saucepan until syrupy *(sweet-salty Malaysian depth)*

        4. Finely dice remaining onion, mince garlic, finely chop chillies

        5. Roughly chop peanuts, reserve ¼ cup for final texture

        6. Make chicken stock with cube and water

    ??? info "Cook (25 minutes)"

        1. Heat 1 tbsp canola oil in large nonstick pan over high heat. Sear chicken in batches until browned but still raw inside *(develops flavor base)*. Transfer to bowl.

        2. Reduce heat to medium, add 1 tbsp oil. Sauté diced onion, garlic, chilli for 2 minutes until onion translucent *(aromatic foundation)*.

        3. Add remaining satay seasoning, cook 1 minute stirring constantly *(blooms spices for maximum flavor)*.

        4. Transfer mixture to blender with ¾ cup chicken stock + ¾ cup peanuts. Blend until smooth *(creates creamy sauce base)*.

        5. Pour back into pan. Add coconut milk/cream *(richness)*, peanut butter *(thickness)*, remaining peanuts *(texture)*, kecap manis *(sweet-salty depth)*, 1 tsp soy sauce *(umami)*, bay leaf *(aromatic)*, MSG *(umami boost)*, and chicken with juices.

        6. Bring to simmer, reduce heat to medium-low. Cook 15 minutes until sauce thickened and chicken cooked through.

        7. Remove bay leaf, add lime juice to taste *(brightness to balance richness)*.

    ??? info "Serve"

        Over basmati rice, garnish with chopped peanuts and parsley.
