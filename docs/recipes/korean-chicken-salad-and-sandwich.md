# Korean-Style Chicken Salad and Sandwich

??? note "Ingredients"

    ??? info "For the chicken"
        - 2 chicken thighs (protein, main component)
        - 2 Tbsp soy sauce (umami, salt)
        - 1 tsp sesame oil (aromatic, nutty depth)
        - 1 tsp minced garlic (flavor depth)
        - 2 Tbsp honey (sweetness, glaze)
        - 1 tsp sriracha (heat, color)
        - 3 Tbsp plain flour (coating base)
        - 3 Tbsp cornflour (crispy texture)
        - Salt and pepper (seasoning)
        - 3 Tbsp oil (for frying)

    ??? info "For the balanced dressing"
        - 1 tsp sriracha (heat)
        - 1 Tbsp soy sauce (saltiness)
        - 1 tsp minced garlic (aromatics)
        - 2 Tbsp apple cider vinegar (brightness)
        - ¼ tsp hoisin paste (sweetness, depth)
        - ½ tsp sesame oil (nuttiness)

    ??? info "For the sriracha mayo"
        - 3 Tbsp kewpie mayo (creamy base)
        - 1 tsp sriracha (heat)
        - ½ tsp honey (balance, sweetness)

    ??? info "For the pickled vegetables"
        - ½ cucumber (coolness, crunch)
        - 3-4 radishes (peppery bite, color)
        - 2 Tbsp apple cider vinegar (acidity)
        - 1 tsp honey (sweetness)
        - ½ tsp salt (preservation)

    ??? info "For assembly"
        - 2 thick slices pane di casa (vessel, texture)
        - 2 handfuls baby spinach (freshness, nutrition)
        - 3-4 lettuce leaves (crunch, freshness)
        - ¼ red onion, thinly sliced (sharp bite, color)

??? note "Preparation"
    Prep Stage (25 minutes)
    1. Thinly slice cucumber and radishes using box grater or knife.
    2. In medium bowl, combine apple cider vinegar, honey, and salt for pickles. Add sliced vegetables and set aside.
    3. Pat chicken thighs completely dry and season with salt and pepper.
    4. Cut one thigh into bite-sized pieces for salad, keep one whole for sandwich.
    5. In medium bowl, mix plain flour and cornflour with pinch of salt.
    6. Make honey glaze by combining 2 Tbsp honey, 1 Tbsp soy sauce, 1 tsp minced garlic, and 1 tsp sriracha in small bowl.
    7. Prepare balanced dressing: Start with sriracha, soy sauce, garlic, and 1 Tbsp vinegar in small bowl. Taste, add remaining vinegar, then hoisin and sesame oil. Adjust to taste.
    8. Make sriracha mayo by combining kewpie mayo, sriracha, and honey in small ramekin.
    9. Wash and dry baby spinach and lettuce leaves.
    10. Slice red onion thinly and set aside.
    Cooking Stage (15 minutes)
    1. Heat oil in wok over medium heat (cannot smoke up the kitchen).
    2. Coat chicken pieces in flour mixture, shaking off excess.
    3. First fry: Cook chicken 4-5 minutes until golden brown all over.
    4. Remove chicken to paper towel-lined plate, drain for 2 minutes.
    5. Second fry: Increase heat to medium-high(cannot smoke up the kitchen), return chicken for 2-3 minutes until extra crispy.
    6. Remove to tray and Immediately brush hot chicken with honey glaze.
    7. Transfer to toaster oven at 180°C fan bake for 5 minutes to ensure cooked through (internal temp 75°C).
    8. Toast pane di casa slices lightly until warmed through but still soft inside.
    Assembly (5 minutes)

    ??? info "For the salad"
        1. In large bowl, combine baby spinach and torn lettuce leaves.
        2. Add thin slices of red onion.
        3. Top with warm glazed chicken pieces.
        4. Arrange pickled vegetables around chicken.
        5. Drizzle balanced dressing over entire salad.
        6. Serve immediately while chicken is warm.

    ??? info "For the sandwich"
        1. Spread sriracha mayo on one slice of bread.
        2. Lightly drizzle balanced dressing on the other slice.
        3. On mayo side: layer lettuce leaves, then glazed chicken thigh.
        4. Top chicken with pickled vegetables.
        5. Close with second slice (dressing side down).
        6. Cut diagonally and serve immediately.
        Enhancements

    ??? info "Technique Improvements"
        - Double-frying technique creates authentic Korean-style crispiness without oil absorption
        - Building dressing in stages prevents over-seasoning with umami ingredients
        - Glazing chicken while hot ensures maximum adhesion and glossy finish
        Notes on Culinary Techniques
        - The combination of hoisin paste and sriracha mimics gochujang's sweet-heat complexity without specialty ingredients
        - Apple cider vinegar's mild fruitiness complements the honey glaze better than harsher white vinegar
        - Cornflour mixed with plain flour creates lighter, crispier coating than flour alone
        - Quick-pickled vegetables (15-20 minutes) retain crunch while developing tangy flavor that cuts through rich glazed chicken
        - Pane di casa's sturdy crumb structure holds up to saucy ingredients better than soft burger buns while adding rustic texture
