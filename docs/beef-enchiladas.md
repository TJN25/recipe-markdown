# Beef Enchiladas with Homemade Red Sauce

**Serves:** 2

??? note "Ingredients"
    ??? info "For the Enchilada Sauce"
        - 2 tablespoons cooking oil (base)
        - 1 onion, finely diced (aromatics)
        - 3 cloves garlic, minced (flavor base)
        - 1½ tablespoons plain flour (thickener)
        - 1½ tablespoons smoked paprika (color, smokiness)
        - 2 teaspoons ground cumin (earthy notes)
        - ½ teaspoon chili powder or cayenne (heat)
        - 1 can (400g) crushed tomatoes (base)
        - 1½ tablespoons tomato paste (richness)
        - 1½ cups chicken stock (liquid)
        - ½ teaspoon sugar (balance)
        - Salt and pepper (seasoning)
    ??? info "For the Beef Filling"
        - 250g beef mince (protein)
        - ½ onion, diced (aromatics)
        - ½ red capsicum, diced (sweetness)
        - 2 cloves garlic, minced (flavor)
        - 1 teaspoon ground cumin (warmth)
        - ½ teaspoon smoked paprika (depth)
        - Salt and pepper (seasoning)
    ??? info "For Assembly"
        - 4-6 tortillas (wrapper)
        - 150g cheese, grated (melting)
        - Sour cream for serving (cooling element)
        - 2 spring onions, chopped (garnish)
        - Fresh coriander, chopped (garnish)
??? note "Method"

    ??? info "Make the Sauce"
        1. Heat oil in large saucepan over medium heat.
        2. Add onion, cook until softened (5-7 minutes).
        3. Add garlic, cook 1 minute until fragrant.
        4. Stir in flour, cook 1 minute (creates roux for thickening).
        5. Add paprika, cumin, and chili powder. Cook 30 seconds until fragrant.
        6. Gradually whisk in stock, then add crushed tomatoes and tomato paste.
        7. Bring to simmer, reduce heat and cook 15-20 minutes until thickened.
        8. Season with sugar, salt, and pepper. Set aside.
    ??? info "Prepare the Filling"
        1. Heat large frypan over medium-high heat.
        2. Brown beef mince, breaking up well.
        3. Add onion, capsicum, and garlic to pan.
        4. Add cumin and paprika, cook until vegetables are soft.
        5. Season well with salt and pepper.
        6. Cook until liquid has reduced and mixture is well-browned.
    ??? info "Assemble and Bake"
        1. Preheat oven to 180°C.
        2. Warm tortillas in dry frypan to make them pliable.
        3. Spread thin layer of sauce in baking dish.
        4. Dip each tortilla lightly in sauce, fill with beef mixture and cheese.
        5. Roll tightly, place seam-side down in dish.
        6. Pour remaining sauce over enchiladas, sprinkle with remaining cheese.
        7. Bake 15-20 minutes until cheese is melted and bubbling.
        8. Rest 5 minutes before serving.
??? note "Notes"

    ??? info "Serving"
        Top with dollops of sour cream, scatter with spring onions and fresh coriander. Serve immediately.

    ??? info "Technical Notes & Enhancements"

        #### Key Techniques
        - Brown meat well for maximum flavor development
        - Don't oversoak tortillas in sauce to prevent tearing
        - Save leftover cheese for quesadillas or toasties
        - Let rest before serving to prevent filling spillage
        #### Flavor Enhancements
        - Add diced jalapeños to filling for extra heat
        - Mix black beans with beef to bulk up filling
        - Add squeeze of lime juice to sauce for brightness
        - Toast spices in dry pan before adding for deeper flavor
        #### Storage & Leftovers
        - Sauce keeps 5 days refrigerated, freezes well
        - Assembled enchiladas keep 3 days refrigerated
        - Reheat covered at 160°C until heated through
        - Extra sauce works great for nachos or quesadillas
        #### Plating Presentation
        - Use warmed white plates to make colors pop
        - Arrange enchiladas diagonally for visual appeal
        - Drizzle sour cream in zigzag pattern
        - Scatter herbs around edges, not center
        - Add lime wedges for color contrast and fresh citrus option
