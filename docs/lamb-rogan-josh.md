# Lamb Rogan Josh (with Extended Cooking Option)

??? note "Ingredients"

    ??? info "For the Marinade"
        - 250g lamb (protein, main ingredient)
        - 2 tbsp Greek yogurt (tenderizer, adds creaminess)
        - 1 tsp garam masala (aromatic spice blend)
        - 1/2 tsp turmeric (color, earthy flavor)
        - 1/2 tsp cayenne pepper (heat)
        - 1 tsp salt (seasoning)

    ??? info "For the Curry"
        - 2 tbsp avocado oil (cooking fat)
        - 1 medium onion, finely diced (aromatic base, sweetness)
        - 3 cloves garlic, minced (aromatic, depth)
        - 1 tsp ginger paste (aromatic, warmth)
        - 1 tsp cumin (earthy flavor)
        - 1 tsp ground coriander (citrusy notes)
        - 1 tsp smoked paprika (depth, smokiness)
        - 2 black cardamom pods, lightly crushed (aromatic)
        - 2 cloves (warm spice note)
        - 1 bay leaf (aromatic)
        - 1/2 can diced tomatoes (acidity, sauce base)
        - 1 lamb stock cube dissolved in 3/4 cup hot water (flavor foundation)
        - 1 tsp dried fenugreek leaves (optional, but adds authentic flavor)
        - Fresh coriander for garnish (optional, to purchase)
        For Extended Cooking (if desired):
        - Extra 1/2 lamb stock cube dissolved in 1 cup hot water (for adding as needed)
        Preparation Instructions
        Prep Stage (15 minutes)
        1. Cut lamb into 2.5cm cubes and place in a medium bowl
        2. Mix yogurt with garam masala, turmeric, cayenne pepper, and salt
        3. Coat lamb pieces thoroughly with the marinade, cover and set aside (ideally for 30 minutes, but even 15 minutes helps)
        4. Finely dice onion and set aside in a small bowl
        5. Mince garlic and measure out ginger paste into a ramekin
        6. Measure all dry spices into another ramekin for easy addition
        7. Dissolve lamb stock cube in hot water and set aside
        Standard Cooking Method (35-40 minutes)
        1. Heat avocado oil in your cast iron pan over medium heat
        2. Add onions and cook until golden brown (about 8-10 minutes), stirring occasionally
        3. Add garlic and ginger paste, cook for 1 minute until fragrant
        4. Add all dry spices (cumin, coriander, smoked paprika, cardamom pods, cloves, bay leaf) and stir for 30 seconds until aromatic
        5. Add marinated lamb and brown on all sides (about 5 minutes)
        6. Add diced tomatoes and lamb stock, stir well
        7. Reduce heat to low, cover with a lid, and simmer for 20-25 minutes until lamb is tender
        8. If using, crush dried fenugreek leaves between your palms and add in the last 5 minutes of cooking
        9. Taste and adjust seasoning if needed
        Extended Cooking Method (1.5-2 hours)
        1. Follow steps 1-6 from the standard method
        2. Reduce heat to very low - aim for a gentle simmer where you see occasional bubbles breaking the surface
        3. Cover with a lid but leave slightly ajar to allow some evaporation
        4. Simmer for 1.5-2 hours, checking every 30 minutes:
        - Stir gently to prevent sticking
        - Add 1/4 cup of the reserved hot lamb stock if the sauce is reducing too much
        - The sauce should gradually thicken and the lamb should become increasingly tender
        5. In the last 15 minutes, crush dried fenugreek leaves between your palms and add to the curry
        6. For the final 5-10 minutes, remove the lid completely to allow the sauce to reduce to your desired consistency
        7. Taste and adjust seasoning if needed
        Serving
        Serve hot with basmati rice. Garnish with fresh coriander if available.
        Enhancement Suggestions
        - Technique Enhancement: For deeper flavor, brown the lamb pieces separately before adding to the curry. This creates a Maillard reaction that adds complexity.
        - Ingredient Enhancement: Adding 1-2 tbsp of butter at the end creates a silkier texture and richer flavor.
        - Spice Enhancement: Toasting whole spices (cumin, coriander) in a dry pan before grinding will intensify their flavors.
        Notes on Techniques and Ingredients
        - Heat Management: For extended cooking, maintaining a very gentle simmer is crucial - too hot and the meat will toughen; too cool and it won't tenderize properly.
        - Doneness Test: The lamb is ready when it can be easily pulled apart with a fork or spoon.
        - Resting Period: If possible, let the curry rest for 15-20 minutes after cooking (with heat off) to allow flavors to meld further.
        - Balancing Heat: The cayenne can be adjusted based on your preference. The current amount gives a moderate heat.
        - Tomato Alternative: If you prefer a redder curry, you could purchase tomato paste (1 tbsp) to enhance the color and flavor.
        - Plating Presentation: Serve in a shallow bowl with rice mounded on one side and curry pooled on the other. Drizzle a small amount of Greek yogurt in a circular pattern and drag a toothpick through it for an elegant design. Finish with a sprinkle of fresh herbs.
        - Leftover Usage: Any leftover curry can be refrigerated and used as a filling for wraps with your tortillas and baby spinach the next day.
