
# Nasi Goreng

**Serves:** 2

??? note "Ingredients"

    ??? info "Chicken"

        - 1.5 tsp oil (fat)
        - 150g chicken breast, thinly sliced (protein, texture)
        - 1 tbsp kecap manis (sweet, salty, umami, caramelisation)

    ??? info "Rice"

        - 1 tbsp oil (fat)
        - 2 garlic clove, finely chopped (aromatic)
        - 1 red chilli, finely chopped (heat, aromatic)
        - 1 small onion, diced (sweetness, aromatic base)
        - 1.5 cups cooked white rice, day old, cold (dry surface for caramelisation)
        - 1 tbsp kecap manis (sweet, salty, umami, colour, caramelisation)
        - 1.5 tsp fish sauce (umami, salty, funk – replaces shrimp paste)
        - Small pinch MSG (umami boost)

    ??? info "Garnishes"

        - 2 eggs, fried sunny side up
        - 1 green onion, greens only, sliced (freshness, colour)
        - Cucumber, sliced (freshness, cuts through richness)
        - Fried shallots (texture)
        - Lime wedges (acidity, brightness)

??? note "Preparation"

    1. Ensure rice is cold from refrigerator – break up any clumps with your hands

    2. Slice chicken breast thinly (about 5mm thick) against the grain

    3. Finely chop garlic and chilli 

    4. Dice onion

    5. Slice green onion greens, set aside

    6. Slice cucumber, set aside

??? note "Method"

    ??? info "Cook Chicken (4-5 minutes)"

        1. Heat wok over high heat

        2. Add 1.5 tsp oil, swirl to coat

        3. Add sliced chicken, spread out in single layer

        4. Cook for 1-2 minutes until mostly white on bottom

        5. Add 1 tbsp kecap manis, toss and cook for another 2-3 minutes until chicken is cooked through and caramelised

        6. Transfer chicken to a clean bowl, set aside

    ??? info "Cook Rice (5-6 minutes)"

        1. Return wok to high heat (don't wipe clean – those caramelised bits add flavour)

        2. Add 1 tbsp oil, swirl to coat

        3. Add garlic and chilli, stir rapidly for 10 seconds until fragrant (don't let garlic burn)

        4. Add diced onion, stir-fry for 1 minute until softened and edges start to colour

        5. Return chicken to pan, toss to combine

        6. Add cold rice, breaking up any remaining clumps

        7. Add 1 tbsp kecap manis, fish sauce, and MSG

        8. Stir-fry constantly, pressing rice against hot pan surfaces for 2-3 minutes until sauce is absorbed and rice grains start to caramelise and smell intensely savoury-sweet

??? note "Notes"

    ??? info "Technique"

        - Don't crowd the pan – high heat is essential for proper caramelisation
        - Consider adding a splash of water if rice seems too dry, but only after kecap manis is absorbed

    ??? info "Flavour Boosters"

        - Toast the chilli and garlic slightly longer (15-20 seconds) for deeper flavour

    ??? info "Alternative Proteins"

        - Thinly sliced pork or beef

    ??? info "Notes on Techniques and Ingredients"

        - **High Heat is Essential**: The caramelisation that occurs when rice hits the hot pan creates complex flavours through Maillard reactions. This is what transforms simple fried rice into Nasi Goreng.
        - **Kecap Manis is Key**: This sweet soy sauce gives the dish its distinctive dark colour and sweet-savoury-umami balance.
