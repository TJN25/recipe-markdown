# Chicken Florentine (Modified for 2 servings, no cream, no wine)

??? note "Ingredients"

    ??? info "For dredging"
        - 2 tablespoons all-purpose flour (coating, helps brown)
        - 2 tablespoons grated Parmesan (umami, salty, helps brown)
        - Salt and black pepper (seasoning)

    ??? info "For chicken"
        - 2 thin-cut boneless skinless chicken breasts (about 225g total) (protein)
        - 1½ teaspoons olive oil (fat, prevents butter burning)
        - 3 tablespoons butter, divided (fat, richness, flavor carrier)

    ??? info "For sauce"
        - 1 small shallot, minced (aromatic, sweetness)
        - 2 garlic cloves, minced (aromatic, pungent flavor)
        - ¾ cup chicken broth (liquid, savory base, deglazes)
        - 1½ tablespoons lemon juice or white wine vinegar (acidity, brightness, cuts richness)
        - ½ teaspoon dried basil (herbal, aromatic)
        - ½ teaspoon dried oregano (herbal, earthy)
        - ¼ cup trim milk (liquid, body)
        - 1-1½ teaspoons cornflour (thickener, creates silky texture)
        - 30g cream cheese, at room temperature (richness, thickener, tangy)
        - 1 cup packed baby spinach (about 45g) (freshness, color, texture, nutrition)
        - Extra Parmesan for serving (umami, salty finish)

??? note "Preparation"

    ??? info "Prep (do this first)"
        1. Take cream cheese out to reach room temperature
        2. Mince shallot and garlic, keep separate
        3. Measure broth and lemon juice into same container
        4. Measure dried herbs together
        5. Wash and dry spinach
        6. Pat chicken breasts completely dry with paper towels
        7. Set up dredging station: mix flour, Parmesan, ½ teaspoon salt, and ¼ teaspoon black pepper on a plate
        8. Have all ingredients within reach of stovetop

??? note "Cooking Instructions"

    ??? info "Step 1: Dredge chicken"
        Coat each chicken breast in the flour mixture, pressing gently so it adheres on both sides. Shake off excess. Set aside on a clean plate.

    ??? info "Step 2: Brown chicken"
        Heat Dutch oven over medium heat for 2-3 minutes. Add olive oil and 1½ tablespoons butter, swirl to combine. When butter foam subsides, add chicken. Cook without moving until golden brown, about 4 minutes. Flip and cook another 4 minutes (won't be cooked through yet). Remove to a plate.

    ??? info "Step 3: Cook aromatics"
        Add another ½ tablespoon butter to the pan. Add shallot and a pinch of salt. Cook, stirring, until softened, about 1½ minutes. Add garlic and cook until fragrant, about 30 seconds.

    ??? info "Step 4: Build sauce"
        Add broth, lemon juice, basil, and oregano. Stir well, scraping all the browned bits from the bottom of the pan. Let bubble and reduce by about half, 3-4 minutes.

        In a ramekin, whisk together trim milk and 1 teaspoon cornflour until completely smooth (no lumps).

        Reduce heat to medium-low. Add cream cheese to the pan in small pieces, stirring until melted and incorporated, about 1-2 minutes. Add the milk mixture and remaining 1 tablespoon butter. Stir continuously until sauce thickens enough to coat the back of a spoon, about 4-6 minutes. If sauce seems too thin, mix another ½ teaspoon cornflour with 1 tablespoon milk and stir in.

        Add spinach and stir until wilted, about 1 minute.

    ??? info "Step 5: Finish chicken"
        Return chicken breasts and any accumulated juices to the pan. Spoon sauce over top. Simmer gently until chicken reaches 75°C internal temperature (use your food thermometer), about 4-5 minutes.

    ??? info "Step 6: Serve"
        Remove from heat. Serve immediately with freshly grated Parmesan on top.

??? note "Notes"

    ??? info "Serving Suggestions"

        - Crusty bread for sopping up sauce
        - Mashed potatoes made in your large saucepan while chicken rests
        - Simple side salad

    ??? info "Storage"

        - Refrigerate leftovers in an airtight container for up to 3 days
        - Reheat gently in a pan over low heat with a splash of broth or milk to loosen sauce
        - Don't freeze - the cream cheese sauce doesn't freeze well

    ??? info "Notes on Technique"

        - **Temperature control is critical** once milk is added - too high and it will curdle or scorch
        - **Cornflour must be whisked into cold liquid** before adding to hot pan to prevent lumps
        - **Room temperature cream cheese** melts smoothly; cold cream cheese will take longer and may leave lumps
        - **Don't skip deglazing** - those browned bits are pure flavor
        - **Chicken carryover cooking** - it will continue cooking slightly after you return it to the sauce, so don't overcook in step 2

    ??? info "Why These Substitutions Work"

        - Trim milk + cornflour + extra butter replaces the thickening and richness of heavy cream at lower fat content
        - Lemon juice + extra broth replaces wine's acidity and liquid volume without alcohol
        - Cream cheese still provides plenty of richness and body to the sauce
