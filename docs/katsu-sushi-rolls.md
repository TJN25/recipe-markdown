
??? note "KATSU SUSHI ROLLS (2 rolls: 1 standard teriyaki, 1 cream cheese sriracha)"

    ??? info "Ingredients"

        **Sushi Rice:**
        - 3/4 cup uncooked short-grain sushi rice *(starchy, sticky)*
        - 18 ml rice vinegar *(sour, preservative)*
        - 1 tbsp sugar *(balances acidity, adds shine)*
        - Pinch salt *(enhances flavour)*

        **Chicken Katsu:**
        - 120 g chicken thigh, skin removed *(fat)*
        - Salt and pepper *(seasoning)*
        - Plain flour, ~3 tbsp *(first coating; helps egg adhere)*
        - 1 egg, beaten *(binder)*
        - Panko breadcrumbs, ~1/2 cup *(crispy coating)*
        - Neutral oil for shallow frying *(vegetable or rice bran; enough for ~1 cm depth in pan)*

        **Teriyaki Glaze:**
        - 2 tsp sugar *(sweet, caramelises)*
        - 2 tsp mirin *(sweet, glossy)*
        - 1 tbsp soy sauce *(salty, umami)*
        - 2 tsp water *(thins glaze)*
        - 1/2 tsp cornflour *(thickener)*

        **For Assembly:**
        - 2 nori sheets *(umami, structure; shiny side faces down when rolling)*
        - 1/2 cup purple cabbage, very finely shredded *(crunch, colour)*
        - 2 tbsp cream cheese, softened *(for roll 2 only: rich, creamy)*
        - Sriracha mayo *(for roll 2 only: 1 tbsp kewpie mayo + 1/2 tsp sriracha + tiny drizzle honey, mixed)*

    ??? info "Preparation"

        **Cook the sushi rice (do this first):**
        1. Rinse rice under cold water in a fine mesh strainer, swishing with your hand, until water runs mostly clear (3–4 rinses). This removes excess starch.
        2. Cook rice according to packet instructions or in your rice cooker with a 1:1 ratio of rice to water (for 3/4 cup rice, use 3/4 cup + 1 tbsp water). Let it steam with the lid on for 10 minutes after cooking finishes.
        3. While rice cooks, mix rice vinegar, sugar, and salt in a small bowl or ramekin. Stir until sugar dissolves (microwave 10 seconds if needed to help dissolve).
        4. When rice is done, transfer hot rice to a large metal bowl. Drizzle the vinegar mixture evenly over the rice.
        5. Use a rice paddle or wooden spoon to fold the vinegar through gently (slicing and lifting motions, not stirring, to avoid mashing grains). Fan the rice with a piece of cardboard or a fan to cool it quickly while folding—this gives it a glossy finish.
        6. Cover bowl with a damp tea towel and set aside at room temperature until ready to roll (use within 2 hours for best texture).

        **Make the chicken katsu:**
        1. Pat chicken thigh dry. If uneven thickness, place between two sheets of cling wrap and gently pound with a rolling pin or the base of a small saucepan to an even 1–1.5 cm thickness.
        2. Season both sides with salt and pepper.
        3. Set up a crumbing station on the large chopping board: shallow bowl or plate with flour, shallow bowl with beaten egg, shallow bowl or plate with panko breadcrumbs (line them up in that order).
        4. Coat chicken in flour, shake off excess. Dip in egg, letting excess drip off. Press into panko, turning to coat both sides evenly. Pat gently so crumbs adhere well. Set aside on a clean plate.
        5. Heat ~1 cm depth of oil in the large nonstick or cast iron pan over medium-high heat (test with a panko crumb—it should sizzle immediately when dropped in).
        6. Carefully place chicken in the hot oil. Fry without moving for 3–4 minutes until the underside is deep golden brown.
        7. Flip with tongs. Fry the other side for 3–4 minutes until golden and the internal temperature reaches 75°C (use food thermometer).
        8. Transfer to a plate lined with paper towel to drain. Let rest 5 minutes (this keeps it juicy and makes slicing easier).

        **Make the teriyaki glaze:**
        1. Combine sugar, mirin, soy sauce, water, and cornflour in a small ramekin. Whisk with a fork until cornflour dissolves.
        2. **Stovetop method:** Pour into the very small saucepan. Heat over medium, stirring constantly, until it thickens and becomes glossy (1–2 minutes). Remove from heat.
           **Microwave alternative:** Microwave on high for 30 seconds, stir, then microwave in 10-second bursts, stirring after each, until thickened (total ~1 minute).

        **Prepare katsu for rolling:**
        1. Brush one side of the rested katsu generously with teriyaki glaze.
        2. Slice katsu into strips roughly 1 cm wide and the length of the thigh (lengthwise cuts work best).

        **Prepare other fillings:**
        1. Shred purple cabbage very finely (use a sharp knife or box grater). If it looks wet, blot lightly with paper towel.
        2. Mix sriracha mayo in a small ramekin: 1 tbsp kewpie mayo + 1/2 tsp sriracha + tiny drizzle honey. Transfer to a squeeze bottle or keep in ramekin for drizzling.
        3. Soften cream cheese by leaving it at room temperature for 10 minutes, or microwave 5 seconds. You want it spreadable but not melted.

    ??? info "Assembly"

        **Set up your rolling station:**
        1. Place a damp tea towel under a chopping board or bamboo mat (if you have one) to stop it sliding.
        2. Have ready: a small bowl of water, sushi rice, nori sheets, katsu strips, cabbage, cream cheese, sriracha mayo, a sharp knife, and a damp cloth for wiping the knife.

        **Roll 1: Standard Teriyaki Katsu Roll**
        1. Place one nori sheet on the board, shiny side down, with the lines running horizontally.
        2. Wet your hands lightly. Grab about half the sushi rice (~1 cup cooked). Spread it evenly over the nori, leaving a 2 cm strip bare at the top edge. Press gently to flatten the rice to about 5 mm thick—don't compact it hard or the roll will be dense.
        3. Arrange 2–3 katsu strips horizontally across the centre of the rice, slightly below the middle.
        4. Add a thin line of shredded purple cabbage next to the katsu.
        5. Lift the bottom edge of the nori with both hands. Fold it up and over the filling, tucking it tightly under the katsu to form a log shape. Keep gentle pressure as you roll upward, using the board or mat to help shape it into a tight cylinder.
        6. When you reach the bare nori strip, wet it lightly with a finger dipped in water. Finish rolling and press gently to seal.
        7. Let the roll rest seam-side down for 1 minute to seal fully.

        **Roll 2: Cream Cheese Sriracha Katsu Roll**
        1. Place second nori sheet on the board, shiny side down.
        2. Spread remaining rice over nori as above, leaving 2 cm bare at the top.
        3. Spread a thin, even layer of cream cheese over the rice (use a butter knife or the back of a spoon—aim for barely visible, not thick).
        4. Arrange remaining katsu strips horizontally across the centre.
        5. Add a line of purple cabbage next to the katsu.
        6. Roll as described above, wetting the bare strip and sealing.
        7. Let rest seam-side down.

        **Slice the rolls:**
        1. Use a very sharp knife (the large chef knife works well). Wet the blade with a damp cloth.
        2. Trim the rough ends off each roll (about 5 mm on each side—these are the "chef's snack").
        3. Slice each roll into 4 equal pieces (cut in half, then cut each half in half). Wipe the knife with the damp cloth between every cut to prevent sticking and tearing.
        4. Stand pieces upright on a serving plate, spiral side showing.

        **Finish Roll 2:**
        1. Drizzle sriracha mayo over the cream cheese roll pieces in a zigzag pattern (use the squeeze bottle or drizzle from a spoon). Don't drown them—a light drizzle adds flavour without overpowering.
