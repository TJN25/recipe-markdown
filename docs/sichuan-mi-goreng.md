# Sichuan Mi Goreng

(1-2 servings)

??? note "Ingredients"
    Sauce Mix
    - 1.5 tbsp soy sauce (umami base)
    - 1 tbsp sriracha sauce (heat, garlic notes)
    - 1.5 tsp brown sugar (balance, sweetness)
    - 1 tbsp white vinegar (acid brightness)
    - ¼ chicken stock cube in 2 tbsp very hot water (depth)
    Aromatics & Heat
    - 3 cloves garlic, minced (base flavor) OR 2-3 tsp minced garlic
    - 2-3 bird's eye chilis, finely chopped (fresh heat) OR 1.5 tsp chili powder
    - ½ onion, finely diced (sweetness)
    - 2 spring onions, whites and greens separated (layered flavor)
    - 1 tsp Sichuan peppercorns, crushed (numbing heat, citrus notes)
    Core Ingredients
    - Egg noodles (1-2 servings)
    - 1 egg (for frying)
    - 2 tbsp canola oil for stir-frying
    - 1 tbsp sesame oil (aromatic finish, glossy coating)
    Prep Stage
    1. Sauce Prep
    - Dissolve stock cube in very hot water
    - Mix in soy sauce, Maggi seasoning, sriracha, sugar, and vinegar
    - Taste for balance and set aside
    2. Aromatics Prep
    - Mince garlic
    - Chop chilis (small plastic board)
    - Dice onion
    - Slice spring onions (separate whites/greens)
    - Crush Sichuan peppercorns in mortar and pestle
    3. Noodle Prep
    - Bring water to boil in medium saucepan
    - Cook noodles until just underdone (1-2 minutes less than package instructions)
    - Reserve ¼ cup cooking water
    - Drain, rinse briefly with cold water to stop cooking
    - Toss with a tiny bit of sesame oil to prevent sticking
    Cooking Stage (Wok)
    1. Heat wok over high heat until smoking
    2. Add 1 tbsp canola oil, swirl to coat sides
    3. Crack egg into wok, let it start to set (about 30 seconds)
    4. Flip egg and cook for another 15-20 seconds until whites are set but yolk is still runny
    5. Remove egg to a plate, set aside
    6. Add remaining 1 tbsp canola oil to wok
    7. Add crushed Sichuan peppercorns, stir for 10 seconds until fragrant
    8. Add garlic, chilis, onion, spring onion whites
    9. Stir-fry until onions are translucent (1-2 minutes)
    - Keep ingredients moving constantly
    - Garlic should be fragrant but not dark
    10. Add noodles and sauce mixture
    - Keep heat high
    - Toss continuously using tongs or chopsticks
    - Sauce should reduce slightly and coat noodles
    11. If noodles seem dry, add 1-2 tbsp of reserved noodle water
    12. Drizzle in remaining sesame oil
    13. Toss for 1 more minute until noodles are glossy with oil coating

??? note "Final Assembly"
    1. Transfer noodles to serving bowl
    2. Slice fried egg in half
    3. Place egg on top of noodles
    4. Garnish with spring onion greens
    5. Serve immediately

??? note "Technical Notes"
    Heat Management
    - The key to the oily coating is proper heat management - keep the wok very hot
    - The sesame oil added at the end creates the glossy coating that helps seasonings stick
    - Sichuan peppercorns infuse the oil with their numbing quality
    Texture Control
    - Undercooking the noodles slightly ensures they don't become mushy during stir-frying
    - The brief cold water rinse stops cooking but doesn't wash away all starch (which helps sauce adhere)
    Flavor Building
    - Maggi seasoning adds depth similar to MSG without adding it directly
    - Sriracha provides both heat and garlic notes in one ingredient
    - Sichuan peppercorns create the distinctive numbing sensation (ma la) characteristic of Sichuan cuisine
    Plating Suggestion
    - Serve in a wide, shallow bowl to showcase the glossy noodles
    - Position the halved fried egg with yolk facing up for visual appeal
    - Scatter spring onion greens in a deliberate pattern rather than just dumping them on
    Variations
    - For extra protein: add 100g diced bacon or pancetta at the beginning of stir-frying
    - For more vegetables: add a handful of baby spinach in the final minute of cooking
    - For extra crunch: top with crushed cashews or peanuts
