# Seasoned Sushi Rice for Poke Bowl

**Serves:** 1-2

??? note "Ingredients"

    ??? info "Rice Base"

        - 1 cup sushi rice (starch, texture - provides sticky, cohesive base with slight natural sweetness)
        - 1¼ cups water (cooking medium)

    ??? info "Seasoning Mix"

        - 1-1½ tablespoons mirin (sweet, umami - adds gentle sweetness and depth without overpowering)
        - 1½ teaspoons white wine vinegar (acid - brightens and balances sweetness)
        - ½ teaspoon soy sauce (salty, umami - adds savory depth and complexity)
        - ½ teaspoon brown sugar (sweet - rounds out sweetness and adds subtle molasses notes)
        - Small pinch of salt (salty - enhances and ties all flavors together)

    ??? info "Optional Enhancement"

        - ¼ teaspoon sesame oil (aromatic, nutty - adds depth)
        - 1 teaspoon toasted sesame seeds (texture, nutty flavor, visual appeal)

??? note "Method"

    ??? info "Prep (5 minutes)"

        1. Rinse sushi rice in fine mesh strainer under cold water, gently swirling and rubbing grains between your fingers, until water runs mostly clear (2-3 rinses). This removes excess surface starch for better texture and prevents gummy rice.

        2. In a ramekin, combine mirin, white wine vinegar, soy sauce, brown sugar, and salt. Stir with a spoon until sugar and salt dissolve completely. Set aside at room temperature.

        3. If using sesame seeds: Toast in small dry pan over medium heat for 2-3 minutes, shaking frequently, until fragrant and lightly golden. Set aside.

    ??? info "Cooking (25 minutes)"

        1. Add rinsed rice and water to medium saucepan. Place over high heat uncovered.

        2. Bring to a full boil (you'll see rapid bubbling across the surface).

        3. Immediately reduce heat to the lowest setting, cover tightly with lid, and simmer for 12 minutes without lifting the lid.

        4. After 12 minutes, remove from heat and let steam, still covered, for 10 minutes. Do not lift the lid during this time (the steaming finishes cooking the rice evenly).

    ??? info "Seasoning (3 minutes)"

        1. After steaming, transfer hot rice to a large metal bowl (the larger surface area helps cooling and mixing).

        2. Pour seasoning mix evenly over the rice.

        3. Using a wooden spoon or plastic spatula, fold the seasoning into rice using a cutting and folding motion - slice down through the rice, then fold over, rotate bowl slightly, repeat. Do not stir in circles or mash (this breaks grains and makes rice mushy).

        4. Continue folding for 1-2 minutes until seasoning is evenly distributed and rice looks glossy.

        5. If using sesame oil, drizzle over rice now and fold in gently. Sprinkle with toasted sesame seeds if using.

        6. Let rice cool to room temperature (about 10 minutes) or use slightly warm for your bowl. Fanning the rice while folding speeds cooling and creates a nice sheen.

??? note "Notes"

    ??? info "Suggestions for Enhancement"

        - **Adjust sweetness to your chicken:** If your teriyaki chicken is very sweet, use 1 tablespoon mirin. If the chicken is more savory or you're using a generous amount of teriyaki drizzle, go with 1½ tablespoons mirin in the rice for more flavor presence.
        - **Sesame oil is worth it:** The ¼ teaspoon addition adds aromatic complexity that bridges beautifully between the rice and teriyaki without being noticeable as "sesame" - it just makes everything taste more cohesive.
        - **Fresh ginger option:** Grate ¼ teaspoon fresh ginger into the seasoning mix for subtle warmth and brightness, especially if you're light on toppings.
        - **Furikake topping:** If you have furikake seasoning, sprinkle some over the rice in the bowl for extra umami, texture, and visual interest without adding more liquid seasoning.
        - **Green onion garnish:** Thinly sliced green onion (white and light green parts) scattered over the rice adds freshness and color contrast against the teriyaki chicken.

??? note "Storage"

    - Use rice within 2 hours at room temperature for food safety.
    - Store leftovers in an airtight container in the refrigerator for up to 2 days.
    - To refresh cold rice: Sprinkle with a few drops of water, cover with a damp paper towel, and microwave for 30-60 seconds. Or let come to room temperature naturally (safer texture-wise).
    - Do not freeze seasoned rice (texture deteriorates significantly).

??? note "Notes"

    ??? info "Notes on Techniques, Ingredients, and Plating"

        - **Technique - Washing rice:** Don't skip this step. Unwashed sushi rice can become gluey and overly sticky. You want sticky enough to hold together for eating, but still with distinct grains.
        - **Technique - The fold, don't stir rule:** This Japanese technique (called "shari-kiri") is used in sushi preparation. Stirring rice in circles smashes the grains, releases more starch, and creates a pasty texture. The cutting and folding motion keeps grains intact while distributing seasoning.
        - **Technique - Season while hot:** Hot rice absorbs seasoning better than cool rice. The moisture and heat help the flavors penetrate. If you season cold rice, it just sits on the surface.
        - **Timing consideration:** Season the rice right before assembling your bowl, or prepare it up to 30 minutes ahead and keep covered with a damp cloth at room temperature. This prevents drying while maintaining the right serving temperature.
        - **Ingredient note - White wine vinegar:** Works well here as a rice vinegar substitute. It's gentler than white vinegar (which can be harsh) and adds the right acidity. In a pinch, you could use apple cider vinegar diluted with a splash of water.
        - **Plating:** For a restaurant-style presentation, pack warm seasoned rice into a ramekin or small bowl, then invert onto your serving bowl to create a neat dome or mound. Alternatively, use the back of a spoon to create a slight well or crater in the center of the rice where you can nestle some of the teriyaki chicken. This creates height and visual interest. Arrange your banh mi pickles alongside or on top of the rice for color contrast - the bright orange carrots and pale cucumber against white rice look striking. Finish with sesame seeds sprinkled over the rice and a drizzle of teriyaki sauce in an artistic pattern across the bowl.
