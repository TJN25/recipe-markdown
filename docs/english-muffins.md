# English Muffins

??? note "Ingredients"
    - ¾ cup bread flour (90g)
    - ½ tsp active dried yeast
    - ¼ tsp salt
    - 1½ tsp sugar
    - 3 tbsp trim milk (45ml)
    - 2 tbsp water (30ml)
    - 2 tsp butter (10g), melted
    - ¼ egg, beaten (about 1 tbsp)
    - All-purpose flour for dusting (as substitute for cornmeal/semolina)
    Oven Method for English Muffins
    Step 1: Prepare the Dough (same as before)
    1. Activate yeast in warm milk, water, and sugar
    2. Mix with flour, salt, egg portion, and melted butter
    3. Use stretch and fold technique instead of kneading
    Step 2: First Rise
    1. Cover and let rise until doubled (about 1 hour)
    Step 3: Shape the Muffins
    1. Surface Choice: Use a well-floured chopping board (wooden is best if available)
    2. Generously flour the board and your hands
    3. Gently scrape the dough onto the floured surface
    4. Dust the top liberally with more flour
    5. Very gently pat down to about 2.5cm (1 inch) thick
    6. Use a floured ramekin to cut out 3-4 muffins
    7. If too sticky to cut cleanly, you can:
    - Flour your hands well and portion the dough into 3-4 equal balls
    - Gently flatten each to form rounds
    Step 4: Second Rise
    1. Line your small baking tray with parchment paper
    2. Dust the parchment generously with flour (as your cornmeal substitute)
    3. Carefully transfer the muffins to the tray, leaving space between them
    4. Dust the tops with more flour
    5. Cover loosely and let rise for 30 minutes
    Step 5: Bake in Oven
    1. Preheat your oven to 180°C (350°F) with convection if available
    2. Bake for 7-8 minutes
    3. Carefully flip the muffins
    4. Bake for another 7-8 minutes until golden and internal temperature reaches 90°C (195°F)
    5. If you don't have a thermometer, tap the bottom - they should sound hollow

    ??? info "Handling Sticky Dough Tips"
        1. Use More Flour: Don't be shy with flour when handling this dough
        2. Oil Your Hands: Lightly oil your hands before handling the dough
        3. Bench Scraper: Use your bench scraper to handle the dough instead of your hands
        4. Cold Water: Keep a small bowl of cold water nearby to dip your fingers in
        5. Rest Time: Let the dough rest 5 minutes after placing on the floured surface before shaping
        Note on Flour vs. Cornmeal/Semolina:
        - The flour dusting will work, though it won't give quite the same texture as cornmeal
        - The muffins may absorb some of the flour during baking
        - You'll still get good English muffins, just with a slightly different exterior texture
        This oven method will be much easier to manage with sticky dough and should produce excellent results!
